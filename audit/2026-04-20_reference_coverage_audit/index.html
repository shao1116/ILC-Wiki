    Reference Coverage Audit

    Created: 2026-04-20

    Scope

    This audit checks whether the ILC-in-lung wiki uses the ingested PDF reference library in a source-aware way across topic pages, digest pages, and entity pages. It does not claim that every PDF has been manually appraised end-to-end. Instead, it assigns every ingested source to a wiki use role so later synthesis can distinguish direct lung evidence, mechanism/context evidence, review/background material, and references that still need manual triage.

    Coverage snapshot

    • Ingested paper source pages in manifest: 121
    • Paper source pages directly linked from topic/digest/entity synthesis pages before this pass: 52
    • Paper source pages present in the library but not yet directly cited in topic/digest/entity pages before this pass: 69
    • All ingested sources remain discoverable through ingestion_manifest and are assigned below for future crystallization.

    Reference-use rules

    • Use lung, airway, pulmonary infection, asthma, COPD, ARDS, fibrosis, and tumor sources as direct support for lung-disease claims only when the source itself studies a relevant lung context.
    • Use gut, skin, nervous-system, colon-cancer, and other extrapulmonary ILC papers as mechanism/context evidence unless a topic page explicitly labels the inference as extrapolated.
    • Use reviews and broad biology papers for framing, terminology, and trend synthesis, not as sole support for a specific mechanistic claim when a primary paper is available.
    • Keep mouse-human, tissue, disease model, and assay distinctions visible in topic pages; do not merge them into a single universal ILC mechanism.
    • Treat automated source pages and automated tags as provisional until manual review. The title-based assignment below is a routing aid, not a final evidence grade.
