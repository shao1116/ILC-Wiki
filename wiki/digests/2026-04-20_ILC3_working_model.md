# ILC3 Working Model

## Scope

This digest organizes the current `ILC_in_lung` evidence base around group 3 innate lymphoid cells (`ILC3s`), with emphasis on pulmonary IL-22/IL-17 biology, mucosal protection, lung development, neutrophilic inflammation, steroid-resistant asthma, and cross-tissue context.

This page is a biological working model for lung and airway ILC3 function. It emphasizes evidence-supported ILC3 claims and preserves species, tissue-compartment, assay, and disease-context boundaries.

## Working model

The lung ILC3 model has three main branches. The first is a human lung baseline branch: human lung tissue contains identifiable ILC3s, including NCR+ and NCR- compartments, and pulmonary ILCs can show inducible IL-17A, IL-22, and GM-CSF potential after stimulation. This establishes ILC3s as a real human pulmonary population while leaving disease causality to disease-specific sources.

The second branch is protective or developmental lung biology. ILC3s can produce IL-22 during Streptococcus pneumoniae infection, linking them to bacterial host defense and barrier-supportive cytokine programs. Neonatal pulmonary ILC3 development is supported by alveolar fibroblast-derived IGF1, making ILC3s relevant to developmental lung immunity rather than only adult inflammation.

The third branch is inflammatory pathology, especially ARDS-like injury, neutrophilic asthma, smoking-associated asthma, and steroid-resistant asthma. The ARDS source places innate lymphoid cells as a major early IL-17A source in acute lung injury models. Smoking asthma sources connect sputum NCR- ILC3s and blood CD45RO+ memory-like ILC3s to neutrophil and M1 macrophage-associated inflammation. Human non-eosinophilic asthma work links ILC3s to neutrophil chemoattractants and glucocorticoid insensitivity, while mouse and human/mouse mechanistic sources add low-dose LPS ILC-required AHR and fibroblast-derived SCF/KIT-driven ILC3 IL-17A augmentation.

For interpretation, the key rule is to avoid treating `ILC3` as automatically beneficial or pathogenic. `IL-22`-dominant ILC3 activity in infection or development should be separated from `IL-17A`/neutrophil-associated ILC3 activity in ARDS, smoke-associated asthma, neutrophilic asthma, and steroid-resistant asthma. Human lung tissue, sputum, blood, nasal airway, and mouse perturbation evidence should remain explicitly labeled.

## Highest-confidence claims

- High confidence: human lung tissue contains identifiable ILC3 subsets, including NCR+ and NCR- compartments, with inducible IL-17A, IL-22, and GM-CSF potential after stimulation.
- High confidence: lung ILC3s can participate in protective or developmental biology through IL-22 during Streptococcus pneumoniae infection and alveolar fibroblast-derived IGF1 support of neonatal pulmonary ILC3 development.
- High confidence: ILC3-associated IL-17A and neutrophil-linked programs are relevant to ARDS-like injury, neutrophilic asthma, smoking-associated asthma, and steroid-resistant asthma, but the strength of causality differs between mouse perturbation and human association/ex vivo studies.
- High confidence: smoking asthma is associated with increased sputum NCR- ILC3s and blood CD45RO+ memory-like ILC3s that align with neutrophil and M1 macrophage signals rather than eosinophils.
- Medium-high confidence: fibroblast-derived SCF/KIT signaling can augment ILC3 IL-17A production and neutrophilic asthma-like inflammation in the reported models.
- Medium confidence: ILC3-related IL-17/neutrophilic pathways are a coherent candidate target space for steroid-resistant asthma, but therapeutic claims require primary intervention evidence.

## Claim-level confidence boundaries

- `High confidence` is used for ILC3 claims supported by direct lung, airway, or pulmonary disease evidence linking ILC3 identity to IL-22, IL-17A, GM-CSF, neutrophil-associated inflammation, or developmental niche activity.
- `Medium-high confidence` is used when a mechanism is supported in reported lung disease models but still needs clearer human causality, compartment mapping, or pathway hierarchy.
- `Medium confidence` is used for therapeutic target claims when the disease biology is coherent but primary intervention evidence in human asthma or lung disease is not yet sufficient.

## Contradictions to track

- `Protection versus pathology`:
  ILC3-derived IL-22 can support antimicrobial defense and barrier biology, while ILC3-linked IL-17A or neutrophil chemoattractants can contribute to lung injury or severe asthma.
- `Tissue context`:
  gut ILC3 mechanisms may not transfer directly to the lung, especially when microbiota, epithelial architecture, and local stromal niches differ.
- `ILC3 versus ILC2 plasticity`:
  some lung disease sources describe ILC2s with ILC3-like signatures or IL-17-producing ILC2-like states. These should not be collapsed into bona fide ILC3s without marker and lineage context.
- `Human association versus mechanism`:
  human asthma blood/sputum ILC3 frequencies and ex vivo function should be distinguished from murine perturbation evidence.
- `ILC versus ILC3 specificity`:
  murine neutrophilic AHR sources can show broad ILC requirement and increased lung ILC3s without proving that ILC3s alone are the causal transferred population.
