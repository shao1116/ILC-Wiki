# ILC2 Niche, Interferon Brake, And Type 2 Regulatory Synthesis

## Scope

This digest integrates ten focused source notes centered on ILC2 tissue positioning, type 2 activation, interferon-mediated brakes, neuroimmune regulation, lung-gut maturation, and type 2 immunity framing. It is organized by biology rather than ingest batch.

## Working Model

The strongest update from this crystallization pass is that lung ILC2s should be modeled as niche-positioned, counter-regulated tissue sentinels. In allergic or helminth-like type 2 contexts, lung ILC2s occupy adventitial/peribronchovascular niches supported by ASC-derived IL-33 and TSLP, respond to epithelial alarmins such as IL-25/IL-33/TSLP, and produce IL-5, IL-13, and amphiregulin-like repair mediators depending on stimulus and tissue state.

The second update is that IFN-gamma is not merely a generic type 1 cytokine in this wiki. Across multiple primary studies, it directly suppresses IL-33-driven ILC2 activation, restrains ILC2 tissue dispersion and survival during mixed inflammation, and can reduce protective ILC2 IL-5/amphiregulin output during influenza. In allergic airway disease, TLR9 activation can use a type I IFN-NK cell-IFN-gamma-STAT1 cascade to suppress ILC2-driven AHR.

The third update is that neuroimmune and tissue-maturation axes diversify ILC2 function. NMU/NMUR1 can drive non-redundant ILC2-derived AREG at barrier surfaces, while PAC1 enables CGRP-mediated inhibitory signaling in ILC2s. Lung and gut ILC2s also show CCR2/CCR4-linked specialization and IL-33-induced inter-organ movement in mice. The review-level type 2 immunity source provides a useful "inside/outside border" framework, but causal claims should remain anchored to the primary studies.

## High-Confidence Claims

- Lung ILC2s are anatomically enriched in adventitial/peribronchovascular niches and interact with IL-33/TSLP-expressing fibroblast-like ASCs.
- Inhaled chitin activates an epithelial alarmin-dependent ILC2 type 2 module and a parallel gamma delta T/IL-17/neutrophil module; ILC2s help drive eosinophil/AAM responses and restrain exaggerated neutrophilic inflammation in that model.
- IFN-gamma can directly suppress IL-33-driven ILC2 activation and can constrain type 2 lymphocyte movement beyond adventitial niches during mixed inflammation.
- In H1N1 influenza, IFN-gamma can suppress protective ILC2 IL-5/amphiregulin output without changing viral load in the reported mouse model.
- TLR9 activation can suppress ILC2-driven AHR through type I IFN, NK-derived IFN-gamma, and ILC2 STAT1 signaling in allergic airway models.
- PAC1/CGRP is an inhibitory neuroimmune ILC2 axis, whereas NMU/NMUR1 is a conserved neuroimmune pathway that can induce non-redundant ILC2-derived amphiregulin at barrier surfaces.
- Lung-gut ILC2 specialization in mice adds a tissue-maturation and inter-organ trafficking layer that should be kept separate from local lung activation.

## Interpretation Boundaries

- Mouse perturbation evidence is strong for mechanism but should not be promoted to human therapy without human disease evidence.
- Review-level framing is useful for conceptual organization, but direct mechanistic confidence comes from primary source notes.
- IFN-gamma can be protective or harmful depending on whether the dominant endpoint is pathogen control, tissue repair, allergic AHR, or mixed-inflammation mortality.
- ILC2 abundance, cytokine output, tissue localization, survival, and disease outcome are separate claim types.
