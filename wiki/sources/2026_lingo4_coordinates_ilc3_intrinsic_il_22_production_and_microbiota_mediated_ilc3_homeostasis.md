## Why It Matters Here

This source adds a cell-intrinsic and microbiota-mediated ILC3 homeostasis mechanism. It is useful for organizing gut ILC3 IL-22 and microbiota circuits that may inspire lung hypotheses, while remaining extrapulmonary unless matched pulmonary data are added.

## Key Findings

- Lingo4-deficient ILC3s showed impaired IL-22 production and altered STAT3-linked signaling after stimulation.
- Lingo4 deficiency was linked to mitochondrial dysfunction, increased ROS/apoptosis, and reduced ILC3 fitness.
- The phenotype included microbiota-dependent effects on ILC3 abundance, with fecal microbiota transfer partially separating genotype-intrinsic and microbiota-mediated components.
- Infection outcomes differed by pathogen context, consistent with IL-22 being protective in some enteric infections but potentially harmful in others.

## Caveats

- Lung relevance is comparator-level only.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: apparent pathogen-specific differences are best handled as context dependence of IL-22 biology.
