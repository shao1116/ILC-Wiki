## Why It Matters Here

This paper is a foundation for the idea that ILCs can regulate adaptive immunity directly, but its durable claim should remain gut-labeled rather than promoted to lung causality.

## Key Findings

- RORgammat-positive ILCs express MHCII and antigen-processing machinery in the reported mucosal compartments.
- These ILCs can process and present antigen, but the observed biological output is restraint of CD4 T-cell proliferation rather than classical priming.
- Selective loss of MHCII in RORgammat-lineage ILCs caused dysregulated commensal-bacteria-dependent CD4 T-cell responses and intestinal inflammation in mice.
- MHCII-positive ILCs were also documented in human mucosal/lymphoid compartments, supporting translational relevance while preserving tissue boundaries.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: MHCII-positive RORgammat-lineage ILCs regulate commensal-specific CD4 T-cell responses in the reported intestinal systems.
- Medium-high confidence: this source supports ILC3 antigen-presentation as an adaptive-immunity restraint mechanism.
- Low confidence: this source should not be used to claim that lung ILC3s use the same MHCII program in pulmonary disease without direct lung evidence.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse intestinal commensal-bacteria models with human mucosal/lymphoid validation.
- Compartment: gut and intestine-draining lymphoid tissue.
- Assay directness: strong for gut CD4 T-cell restraint through ILC MHCII perturbation; indirect for lung disease.
- Best wiki use: ILC3-CD4 T-cell tolerance, MHCII antigen-presentation boundary, and adaptive-immunity overview.

## Caveats

- Keep this source gut-labeled.
- Do not equate MHCII expression with productive T-cell priming; the reported output is mainly restraint.
- Do not merge this mechanism with dendritic-cell antigen presentation unless the source explicitly compares them.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: helps explain why antigen-presentation machinery on ILCs can restrain rather than amplify T-cell responses.
- Supersession status: not superseded; later ILC3-Treg work refines, rather than replaces, this tolerance branch.
