## Why It Matters Here

This source adds a therapeutically relevant cholinergic branch to the ILC2 wiki. It is especially useful because it argues that not every ILC2-modulating therapy acts directly on ILC2s; some interventions work by altering neighboring immune partners that feed into ILC2 activation.

## Key Findings

- In the papain-induced eosinophilic airway-inflammation model, tiotropium reduced airway eosinophils, type 2 cytokines, and ILC2 numbers in bronchoalveolar lavage.
- Tiotropium did not directly suppress IL-33-induced IL-5 and IL-13 production from isolated ILC2s in vitro.
- Basophils expressed the highest M3R signal among the murine immune cells examined in this source.
- Tiotropium suppressed basophil IL-4 production, and the source linked this upstream effect to reduced secondary ILC2 activation.
- The study therefore supports an indirect muscarinic-regulation branch for ILC2-dependent airway eosinophilic inflammation.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: tiotropium reduces papain-driven eosinophilic airway inflammation and associated ILC2 expansion in the reported mouse model.
- High confidence: the source argues against a dominant direct effect of tiotropium on IL-33-stimulated ILC2 cytokine production.
- Medium-high confidence: basophil M3R-dependent IL-4 production is the leading indirect pathway supported here for altered ILC2 activation.
- Low confidence: this source should not be generalized into all asthma therapies or all muscarinic biology in human ILC2s.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse papain-driven airway eosinophilic inflammation.
- Assay directness: strong for intervention and inflammatory phenotype, moderate for pathway hierarchy.
- Best wiki use: therapeutic modulation, indirect cholinergic regulation, and basophil-ILC2 crosstalk in airway inflammation.

## Caveats

- The disease model is papain-driven airway inflammation and does not capture every asthma endotype.
- Evidence for direct ILC2 muscarinic signaling is weaker than evidence for indirect regulation through basophils.
- Translation to human tiotropium response should stay conservative unless paired with dedicated human ILC2 evidence.
