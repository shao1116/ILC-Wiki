## Why It Matters Here

This source broadens the ILC2-macrophage map beyond lung macrophage imprinting. It shows that ILC2 type 2 effector cytokines can remodel a systemic splenic macrophage compartment, but it should not be promoted as direct pulmonary ILC2 evidence.

## Key Findings

- IL-33R signaling was dispensable for steady-state red pulp macrophage development, Pparg expression, erythrophagocytosis, and splenic iron homeostasis in the reported models.
- Recombinant IL-33 expanded splenic red pulp macrophages and increased splenic ST2+ ILC2 accumulation/proliferation.
- IL-33-induced RPM expansion failed in Rag2-/- Il2rg-null lymphocyte-deficient mice and in ILC2-deficient Id2fl/fl;Nmur1Cre mice, but was preserved in Tcrb-deficient mice, supporting an ILC2-dependent and alpha-beta T-cell-independent pathway in the short IL-33 model.
- GM-CSF receptor deficiency partially reduced RPM expansion, while IL-4Ralpha deficiency strongly reduced RPM expansion; IL-4 and IL-13 were sufficient to expand RPMs in vitro.
- Nippostrongylus brasiliensis infection expanded splenic ILC2s and RPMs, but the authors note that infection-stage RPM expansion may involve ILC2s, Th2 cells, and other IL-4/IL-13 or GM-CSF-producing cells.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: elevated IL-33 promotes RPM expansion indirectly through activated ILC2s in the reported short-term mouse IL-33 system.
- High confidence: IL-4Ralpha signaling and GM-CSF contribute to RPM expansion, with IL-4/IL-13 sufficient in vitro and GM-CSF receptor effects partial in vivo.
- Medium-high confidence: this source supports ILC2-macrophage crosstalk as a systemic type 2 immunity principle.
- Low confidence: this source does not establish direct lung disease mechanisms or human relevance.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse recombinant IL-33 treatment, spleen RPM biology, and Nippostrongylus brasiliensis infection.
- Main assays: flow cytometry, EdU proliferation, IL-33/IL-33R/MyD88 deficiency, mixed bone marrow chimeras, Rag2/Il2rg lymphocyte deficiency, Tcrb deficiency, Id2fl/fl;Nmur1Cre ILC2 deficiency, Il4ra and Csf2ra perturbation, RPM culture with IL-4/IL-13/IL-33, and parasite infection.
- Assay directness: strong for splenic RPM expansion; indirect for lung ILC2 disease biology.
- Best wiki use: ILC2 functional regulation, systemic type 2 macrophage crosstalk, and guardrails around extrapolating IL-33/ILC2 effects across tissues.

## Caveats

- Preserve the spleen/systemic context; this is not a lung macrophage source.
- The short recombinant IL-33 model supports ILC2 dependence, whereas nematode infection may involve Th2 cells and other cytokine-producing cells.
- IL-33 is not required for homeostatic RPM development or function in this study, despite promoting RPM expansion when elevated.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: refines IL-33/RPM biology by separating homeostatic RPM development from high-IL-33 inflammatory expansion.
- Supersession status: complements lung ILC2-macrophage niche sources by adding a systemic/splenic macrophage crosstalk branch.
