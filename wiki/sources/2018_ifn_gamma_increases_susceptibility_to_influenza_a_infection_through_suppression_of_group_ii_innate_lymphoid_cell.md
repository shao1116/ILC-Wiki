## Key Findings

- Influenza-induced IFN-gamma suppresses lung ILC2 function after H1N1 CA04 infection.
- IFN-gamma deficiency increases ILC2 IL-5 and amphiregulin production and improves lung tissue integrity without changing ILC2 number, viral load, or viral clearance.
- Anti-IFN-gamma treatment improves survival in an ILC2- and IL-5-dependent manner in the reported mouse system.
- The paper supports separating ILC2 abundance from ILC2 functional output during viral infection.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: in this mouse influenza model, IFN-gamma suppresses protective ILC2 IL-5/AREG-associated function.
- Medium-high confidence: IFN-gamma neutralization improves survival in a source-supported preclinical model, but this is not a clinical recommendation.
- Low confidence: the claim should not be generalized to all influenza strains, time points, or human disease without matching evidence.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse H1N1 influenza infection.
- Assay directness: cytokine neutralization/deficiency and cell-dependence experiments tied to survival and tissue readouts.
- Best wiki use: viral ILC2 repair/protection, IFN-gamma brake, and cell-function versus cell-number distinction.

## Caveats

- IFN-gamma is broadly protective in many infections; this source supports a specific lung ILC2-suppression branch in one influenza model.
- Do not interpret unchanged viral load as meaning IFN-gamma is irrelevant to antiviral immunity in other settings.
