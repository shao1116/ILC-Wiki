## Why It Matters Here

This source makes the ILC2 wiki materially better because it turns "ILC2 niche" from a static location claim into a dynamic lung-navigation model. It links chemokine guidance and extracellular matrix structure to where activated pulmonary ILC2s accumulate, how they explore tissue, and how they contribute to eosinophilic inflammation.

## Key Findings

- In inflamed mouse lung, activated pulmonary ILC2s were highly dynamic and showed amoeboid-like movement in peribronchial and perivascular regions.
- Lung ILC2s expressed chemokine receptors including CCR8, and CCR8 blockade reduced ILC2 accumulation, motility, and type 2 cytokine output in the reported IL-33-driven system.
- CCL8 deposits localized around airway epithelium and the source identified this CCR8-CCL8 axis as a key positional cue for pulmonary ILC2s.
- Collagen-I altered ILC2 shape, polarity, and exploratory behavior, and blocking collagen fibrillogenesis in vivo increased ILC2 motility while reducing eosinophil accumulation in inflamed lung.
- Human ILC2s also migrated in response to CCL8 and extracellular-matrix cues in the reported in vitro migration assays.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: this source supports a murine lung model in which activated ILC2s accumulate in peribronchial and perivascular spaces and move in an amoeboid manner during IL-33-driven inflammation.
- High confidence: CCR8-CCL8 signaling is a source-supported positional and functional cue for pulmonary ILC2 accumulation in the reported mouse system.
- Medium-high confidence: collagen-I and related extracellular-matrix structure regulate ILC2 morphology and motility and influence eosinophilic inflammation in this source.
- Medium-high confidence: human ILC2s respond chemotactically to CCL8 and matrix cues in vitro, but this is not the same as direct in vivo human lung tracking evidence.
- Low confidence: this source should not be used alone to claim that every asthmatic or viral lung context uses the same CCR8-CCL8 or collagen-I ILC2 migration program.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse IL-33 and Alternaria-related lung inflammation plus human ILC2 migration assays.
- Assay directness: unusually strong for spatial biology because the source combines intravital imaging, lung slices, receptor perturbation, and matrix manipulation.
- Best wiki use: spatial niche biology, airway/perivascular positioning, migration cues, and environmentally guided ILC2 function in inflamed lung.

## Caveats

- The strongest causal data are in mouse inflammatory lung models, not in direct human lung imaging.
- Human evidence is mainly chemotaxis and migration behavior rather than tissue-level causality.
- Matrix findings should remain tied to the reported extracellular-matrix context rather than generalized to all fibrotic or remodeled lungs.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: this source complements, rather than contradicts, the adventitial-stromal niche paper by adding a dynamic movement layer to niche positioning.
- Supersession status: not superseded; it remains one of the clearest direct sources for pulmonary ILC2 trafficking and environmental guidance.
