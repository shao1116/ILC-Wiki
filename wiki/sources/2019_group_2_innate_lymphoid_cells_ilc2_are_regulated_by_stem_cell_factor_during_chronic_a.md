## Why It Matters Here

This paper pairs with the 2025 fibroblast SCF/KIT ILC3 source by showing that SCF/c-Kit is also a lung ILC2 regulatory axis in chronic allergic disease. It helps separate ILC2-SCF and ILC3-SCF branches.

## Key Findings

- SCF binds c-Kit, which is expressed by ILC2s in the study context.
- Antibody targeting SCF248 attenuated chronic asthmatic disease and reduced mast cells, ILC2s, eosinophils, and pathogenic cytokine responses.
- SCF conditional and c-Kit-defective models supported the importance of SCF/c-Kit activation during chronic allergy.
- SCF could activate ILC2s in vitro to produce key allergic cytokines.

## Methods and Context

- Chronic allergic airway disease model.

## Caveats

- SCF/c-Kit affects multiple cell types including mast cells and eosinophils, so ILC2-specific causality should be worded carefully.
- Mouse chronic allergy evidence should not be overextended to all human asthma phenotypes.
- Keep separate from the 2025 fibroblast SCF/KIT ILC3 neutrophilic asthma axis.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction/tension: SCF/KIT can regulate ILC2 in type 2 chronic allergy and ILC3 in neutrophilic asthma; these are related but distinct axes.
