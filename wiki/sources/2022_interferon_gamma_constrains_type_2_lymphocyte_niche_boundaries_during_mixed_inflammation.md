## Key Findings

- Type 2 inflammation can drive ILC2 and Th2 expansion beyond adventitial niches into lung and liver parenchyma.
- During mixed type 1/type 2 inflammation, broadly distributed IFN-gamma-producing type 1 lymphocytes directly restrict ILC2 parenchymal trafficking and survival.
- IFN-gamma-mediated confinement of type 2 lymphocytes limits mortality during Listeria monocytogenes infection in the reported model.
- The study connects cytokine cross-regulation, tissue localization, trafficking, and host-defense tradeoffs.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: IFN-gamma directly restricts IL-5+ ILC2/Th2 tissue dispersion and parenchymal accumulation in the reported mouse mixed-inflammation models.
- High confidence: the source supports niche-boundary control as a biological mechanism, not just reduced cytokine production.
- Medium confidence: exact relevance to chronic human lung disease requires matching spatial human data.

## Methods and Context
