## Why It Matters Here

This source defines a core regulatory logic: IL-33 activates ILC2s and Tregs, while IFN-gamma suppresses that type 2/homeostatic pathway during type 1 immune perturbation.

## Key Findings

- IL-33 activates ILC2s and supports Treg accumulation in resting and perturbed tissues.
- ILC2-intrinsic IL-33 responsiveness is required for Treg accumulation in vivo through a mechanism that is independent of ILC2 type 2 cytokines and partly dependent on ICOSL-ICOS costimulation.
- IFN-gamma directly represses ILC2 proliferation and cytokine output and blocks IL-33-driven ILC2 and Treg accumulation during type 1 perturbation.
- The study provides an early mechanistic basis for viewing IFN-gamma as a brake on IL-33-driven ILC2 function.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: IL-33 and IFN-gamma counter-regulate ILC2 activation in the reported mouse systems.
- High confidence: ILC2s can support Treg accumulation through ICOSL/ICOS-linked interactions independent of canonical IL-5/IL-13 output.
- Medium confidence: adipose, helminth, and infection contexts should not be collapsed into one lung-specific claim without explicit tissue labels.
