## Key Findings

- Innate lymphoid cells (ILCs) are predominantly tissue-resident, and accumulating data indicate that they have signi ﬁcant tissue-speciﬁc functions.
- We summarize current knowledge of ILC phenotypes in various tissues in mice and humans, aiming to clarify ILC immunity in distinct anatomical locations.
- Fundamental Characteristics of Innate Lymphoid Cells Innate lymphoid cells (ILCs) comprise non-T, non-B, lymphoid cells in mice and humans.
- A univer- sal nomenclature proposed in 2018 [ 1] segregated ILCs into ﬁve distinct subsets: natural killer (NK) cells, ILC1, ILC2, ILC3, and lymphoid tissue inducer cells (LTi, see Glossary), of which only NK cells and ILC1, the latter in some tissues, demonstrate cytotoxic activity [ 2].
- Although cy- totoxic CD56 dim NK cells lack the interleukin (IL)-7 receptor subunit α (IL-7R-α, also known as CD127), and CD56 bright NK cells express intermediate amounts, ILCs show high expression of CD127 [3,4].
