## Why It Matters Here

This source broadens the receptor-level regulation map for ILC2s. It shows that cannabinoid biology in the lung is not uniformly suppressive and that CB2 can function as a positive amplifier of activated ILC2-driven airway disease.

## Key Findings

- Naive and activated murine pulmonary ILC2s expressed CB2 in the reported experiments.
- CB2 signaling did not measurably alter steady-state ILC2 homeostasis but promoted activated ILC2 proliferation and function.
- CB2 deficiency or antagonism reduced ILC2-driven airway inflammation, whereas CB2 agonism exacerbated airway hyperreactivity and lung inflammation in IL-33, IL-25, and Alternaria models.
- Mechanistically, CB2 signaling induced CREB phosphorylation in ILC2s in the reported system.
- Human ILC2s also expressed CB2, and agonist versus antagonist manipulations had opposing effects in humanized-mouse airway-hyperreactivity experiments.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: CB2 signaling amplifies activated ILC2-driven airway inflammation in the reported mouse models.
- High confidence: CB2 is not simply a homeostatic marker here; it functions as a positive regulator after ILC2 activation.
- Medium-high confidence: CREB phosphorylation is a source-supported downstream signaling feature of the CB2 branch.
- Medium-high confidence: humanized-model data support translational relevance, but direct human lung-tissue causality remains limited.
- Low confidence: this source should not be generalized into all cannabinoid biology or all asthma endotypes.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse allergic airway models plus humanized-model and human ILC2 experiments.
- Assay directness: strong for receptor perturbation and disease phenotype.
- Best wiki use: receptor-level ILC2 amplification, airway hyperreactivity, and noncanonical immune-modulatory ligand framing.

## Caveats

- Most causal evidence is from mouse and humanized systems.
- Cannabinoid signaling is context-dependent and should not be framed as uniformly pro- or anti-inflammatory across all immune cells.
- Human translational claims should remain paired with the model labels used in the source.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: this source is best integrated as a positive activation branch that counterbalances inhibitory dopamine, PAC1/CGRP, and beta2-adrenergic signals.
- Supersession status: not superseded; it remains useful for showing that some receptor families enhance rather than restrain activated ILC2s.
