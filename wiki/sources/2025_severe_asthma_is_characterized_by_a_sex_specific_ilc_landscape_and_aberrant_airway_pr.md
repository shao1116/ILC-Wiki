## Why It Matters Here

This source is a strong human severe-asthma anchor for the wiki because it directly compares peripheral blood and induced sputum ILC profiles while preserving sex, compartment, and biologic-therapy context. It also supports a major interpretation guardrail: surface-marker-only ILC enumeration can miss functional and transitional ILC states in clinical samples.

## Key Findings

- High-parameter flow cytometry showed that conventional c-Kit/CRTH2 surface-marker strategies do not fully resolve human ILC subset identity or function.
- In blood, patients with severe asthma showed increased circulating PLZF+ ILC progenitors at the population level, with strong sex-stratified differences.
- Females with severe asthma showed higher blood ILC/ILCP, ILC1, and ILC3 abundance than matched healthy controls or male severe-asthma comparators in the reported cohort; circulating ILCPs inversely correlated with testosterone.
- Induced sputum from patients with severe asthma showed increased total ILCs, T-bet+ ILC1s, GATA3+ ILC2s, RORgammat+ ILC3s, and PLZF+ ILCPs relative to healthy controls.
- Airway GATA3+ ILC2s correlated positively with ACQ-7 and negatively with FEV1 percent predicted in pre-biologic severe asthma; multisource clustering linked an airway ILC2 signature to worse lung function.
- Anti-IL-5/5Ralpha biologics reduced IL-5+ and IL-13+ airway ILCs but did not reduce the abundance of core ILC subsets in blood or sputum.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: severe asthma in this human cohort is associated with a compartment-specific ILC landscape in blood and induced sputum.
- High confidence: airway, not blood, ILC2 signatures are more directly associated with reduced lung function in the clustering analysis.
- Medium-high confidence: sex stratification is biologically important for interpreting ILC landscapes in severe asthma, especially ILCP, ILC1, and ILC3 signals in blood.
- Medium-high confidence: anti-IL-5/5Ralpha biologics suppress type 2 cytokine-producing airway ILCs without depleting core ILC subset abundance in this cohort.
- Low confidence: this observational human cohort does not prove that ILC changes are causal drivers of severe asthma or therapy response.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: human healthy controls and severe-asthma participants in the PROCAMS cohort.
- Compartments: peripheral blood and induced sputum; sputum analysis was limited by low cell recovery and smaller sample size.
- Therapy context: mepolizumab and benralizumab were analyzed as anti-IL-5/5Ralpha biologic therapy in the reported cohort.
- Assay directness: strong for high-dimensional ILC phenotyping and cytokine readouts; associative for clinical outcomes and therapy-response interpretation.
- Best wiki use: human severe-asthma ILC landscape, blood-versus-airway compartment interpretation, sex dimorphism, ILC2-lung function association, and anti-IL-5/5Ralpha biologic effects on airway ILC cytokine output.

## Caveats

- Do not treat blood ILC frequencies as interchangeable with airway ILC states.
- Sex-stratified conclusions are strongest for blood; sputum sex-stratified analysis was limited by sample availability.
- Mepolizumab and benralizumab were grouped for most analyses; biologic-specific effects require larger treatment-arm-specific studies.
- PMA/ionomycin stimulation and high-dimensional flow support functional profiling but do not establish in vivo cytokine production rates.
- The source supports human association and therapy-response framing, not direct causal proof that a specific ILC subset drives severe asthma.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: helps explain inconsistent clinical ILC2 reports by emphasizing compartment, sex, and gating strategy.
- Supersession status: strengthens human severe-asthma ILC interpretation but does not replace mechanistic mouse or sputum-specific severe-asthma sources.
