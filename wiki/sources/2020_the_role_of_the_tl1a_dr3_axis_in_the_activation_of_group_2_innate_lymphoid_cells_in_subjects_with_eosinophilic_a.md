## Key Findings

- Abstract Rationale: Group 2 innate lymphoid cells (ILC2s) are critical for type 2 inﬂammation.
- In murine models of asthma, some ILC2s remain activated in the absence of epithelial cell–derived cytokine signaling, implicating alternate stimulatory pathways.
- DR3 (death receptor 3), a member of the tumor necrosis factor receptor superfamily, is expressed on ILC2s.
- Genome-wide association studies report an association between DR3 ligand, TL1A (tumor necrosis factor–like protein 1A), and chronic inﬂammatory conditions.
- Objectives: We investigated the TL1A/DR3 axis in airway ILC2 biology in eosinophilic asthma.
