## Why It Matters Here

This source is important because it supplies direct human airway evidence for an alternative activating pathway in eosinophilic asthma. It helps the wiki avoid reducing ILC2 activation to IL-33/IL-25/TSLP alone and links severe eosinophilic disease to a monocyte-TL1A-airway ILC2 circuit.

## Key Findings

- Allergen challenge in mild asthma increased sputum DR3-positive ILC2s at 24 hours in the reported cohort.
- IL-2, IL-33, and TSLP upregulated DR3 expression on human ILC2s in vitro.
- TL1A increased IL-5 expression by ILC2s, and dexamethasone attenuated this effect unless TSLP was present.
- Airway TL1A increased after allergen challenge and was highest in subjects with prednisone-dependent severe eosinophilic asthma, especially in the subgroup with airway autoimmune features.
- Immune complexes from severe-asthma sputum stimulated monocyte TL1A production in the reported experiments.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: the TL1A/DR3 axis is a source-supported activation pathway for human airway ILC2 biology in eosinophilic asthma contexts.
- High confidence: allergen challenge and type 2 cytokine context increase DR3-positive ILC2 representation in sputum or in vitro human ILC2 assays.
- Medium-high confidence: TSLP can blunt steroid restraint of TL1A-driven ILC2 activation in the reported in vitro setting.
- Medium-high confidence: severe eosinophilic asthma with autoimmune features is linked here to higher airway TL1A, but this subgroup association should remain source-specific.
- Low confidence: this source should not be generalized to all asthma endotypes or all lung ILC2 activation outside eosinophilic-airway contexts.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: human sputum and allergen-challenge asthma cohorts plus in vitro human ILC2 assays.
- Assay directness: strong for human airway association and ILC2 activation biology; limited for deep tissue causality.
- Best wiki use: eosinophilic asthma, airway ILC2 activation, steroid-context modulation, and monocyte-ILC2 inflammatory circuitry.

## Caveats

- The strongest evidence is airway/sputum-focused and should not be rewritten as pan-lung parenchymal causality.
- Severe-asthma subgroup findings need to stay tied to the reported cohort structure.
- TL1A/DR3 should be positioned as one branch of ILC2 activation, not as a universal master pathway.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: this source extends epithelial-alarmin models rather than contradicting them.
- Supersession status: not superseded; it remains a valuable human airway source for non-alarmin ILC2 activation in eosinophilic asthma.
