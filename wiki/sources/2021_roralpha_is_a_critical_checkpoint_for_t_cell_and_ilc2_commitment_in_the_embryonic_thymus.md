## Key Findings

- Specific lineage commitment is dependent on the interplay of discrete stromal microenvironments, providing intercellular signals via cell contact and cytokines and the induction of lineage-restricted transcription factors (TFs).
- Although the thymus provides a specialized environment that promotes T cell differentiation, T cell progenitors in the adult thymus retain ILC2 potential until the DN2 stage7 and also produce natural killer (NK) cells8.
- The presence of ILC2-like cells was noted in adult and embryonic thymi 9,10, but the development of ILC2 cells from ETPs in vivo remains to be demonstrated, as do the critical factors that distinguish T cell and ILC2 fates.
