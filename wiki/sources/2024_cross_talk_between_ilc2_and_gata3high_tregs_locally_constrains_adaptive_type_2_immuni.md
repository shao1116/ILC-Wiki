## Evidence Profile

- Overall confidence: high for source-specific mouse lung evidence that ILC2s help recruit/expand Gata3high Tregs through CCL1-CCR8 and OX40L-OX40-linked dialogue, and that these Tregs restrain adaptive type 2 immunity by limiting OX40L availability and effector-memory Th2 expansion.

- Primary biological axis: IL-33-activated ILC2s provide OX40L and CCL1-linked signals that support local Gata3high Tregs; Gata3high Tregs feed back on ILC2 OX40L availability to restrain effector/memory Th2 expansion and allergic lung inflammation.

## Why It Matters Here

This source sharpens the lung ILC2-adaptive-immunity map by showing that ILC2 costimulation is not only pro-inflammatory. In a type 2 inflamed niche, ILC2s can also enable a Treg feedback arm that limits effector-memory Th2 expansion.

## Key Findings

- IL-33 or allergen exposure expanded lung ILC2s, Gata3high Th2 cells, and Gata3high Tregs; spatial and two-photon imaging placed Tregs and Th2 cells near ILC2-rich adventitial/peribronchovascular niches.
- Bulk RNA-seq and receptor-ligand analysis identified Ccr8, Tnfrsf4/OX40, and tissue-residency features in Gata3high Tregs; Ccr8 deficiency impaired IL-33-driven Gata3high Treg expansion.
- ILC2-deficient mice showed impaired tissue Treg expansion after IL-33, and ILC2-linked OX40L, rather than ICOSL, was required for Gata3high Treg expansion in the tested IL-33 setting.
- Treg-intrinsic OX40 deletion impaired Gata3high Treg expansion in lung and other tissues after IL-33 exposure without broadly disrupting baseline Treg suppressive function.
- After Alternaria or papain challenge, OX40 Treg-conditional knockout mice showed exaggerated eosinophilic/type 2 lung inflammation, reduced relative Gata3high Treg enrichment, increased Th2/Treg ratios, and amplified effector-memory Th2 cells in mediastinal lymph nodes.
- Gata3high Tregs directly reduced OX40L surface availability on IL-33-stimulated ILC2s in coculture, and anti-OX40L treatment reduced the amplified effector-memory Th2 phenotype and allergic inflammation in OX40 Treg-conditional knockout mice.
- Human lung cancer scRNA-seq analysis identified GATA3high Treg subsets expressing TNFRSF4, CCR8, CXCR6, and GATA3, supporting a cross-species comparator but not proving the mouse circuit in human asthma.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: in mouse IL-33 and allergen lung models, ILC2-Treg dialogue through OX40L/OX40 costimulation and CCL1/CCR8-linked chemotaxis supports local Gata3high Treg accumulation.
- High confidence: loss of Treg-intrinsic OX40 causes exaggerated adaptive type 2 inflammation and effector-memory Th2 expansion in the reported mouse airway models.
- Medium-high confidence: Gata3high Tregs can tune ILC2 OX40L availability, making the ILC2-OX40L pathway a bidirectional feedback circuit rather than a one-way Th2 amplifier.
- Medium confidence: human scRNA-seq Treg states provide translational plausibility for GATA3high CCR8+ OX40+ Tregs in lung tissue, but not direct functional proof in human asthma.
- Low confidence: this source should not be used to claim that all OX40/OX40L targeting will suppress type 2 disease; the effector and regulatory arms have opposite consequences depending on which cell is engaged.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse C57BL/6-based IL-33, papain, Alternaria, house dust mite, and ragweed type 2 inflammation systems; human lung cancer scRNA-seq dataset used for Treg-state comparison.
- Main compartments: lung, airways/BAL, mediastinal lymph node, and selected extrapulmonary tissues for IL-33-driven Treg expansion.
- Main assays: flow cytometry, multiplex immunofluorescence, two-photon imaging, bulk RNA-seq, CellChat receptor-ligand analysis, TCR diversity analysis, reporter mice, ILC2-deficient systems, Ccr8 deficiency, Treg-conditional Tnfrsf4/OX40 deletion, ILC2 OX40L perturbation, Treg depletion, ILC2-Treg coculture, anti-OX40L neutralization, and allergic lung inflammation readouts.
- Best wiki use: lung ILC2-Treg feedback, adaptive type 2 restraint, OX40L bioavailability, Gata3high Treg niche adaptation, and caution around broad OX40/OX40L therapeutic interpretation.

## Caveats

- The main causal evidence is mouse lung/allergen/IL-33 biology; the human component is a public lung cancer scRNA-seq comparator, not a human asthma perturbation.
- OX40L has divergent effects on effector and regulatory adaptive type 2 arms: ILC2 OX40L can support Th2 responses, but it also supports Gata3high Tregs that restrain those responses.
- Gata3high Tregs are described as a functional tissue-adapted Treg state; they should not be collapsed into all Tregs or used as a generic regulatory-cell label.
- ILC2s are positioned centrally in this circuit, but CD4 T cells remain key mediators of antigen-recall type 2 inflammation in the allergen models.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: resolves an apparent paradox in which ILC2 costimulation can both amplify and restrain adaptive type 2 immunity, depending on whether Th2 cells or Gata3high Tregs dominate the downstream effect.
- Supersession status: extends the 2018 ILC2-OX40L lung costimulation source by adding Treg-intrinsic OX40 and feedback control of OX40L availability.
