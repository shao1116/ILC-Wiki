## Why It Matters Here

This source strengthens the ILC3-MHCII tolerance branch by linking ILC3 antigen presentation to selection of commensal-specific CD4 T cells and intestinal inflammation.

## Key Findings

- MHCII-positive ILC3s directly interacted with activated commensal-specific CD4 T cells in the reported systems.
- The source supports induction of death or deletion-like restraint in activated commensal-specific CD4 T cells rather than broad T-cell activation.
- ILC3-intrinsic MHCII loss caused spontaneous CD4 T-cell-dependent intestinal inflammation in mice.
- Reduced MHCII expression on colonic ILC3s in pediatric IBD supports disease relevance, but remains gut/IBD context.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: intestinal ILC3 MHCII constrains commensal-specific CD4 T-cell responses in the reported models.
- Medium-high confidence: this source supports ILC3s as adaptive-immunity gatekeepers in mucosal tolerance.
- Low confidence: direct pulmonary extrapolation is not justified without matched lung ILC3-MHCII data.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse commensal-specific CD4 T-cell systems with pediatric IBD tissue association.

## Caveats
