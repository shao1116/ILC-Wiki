## Key Findings

- PAC1 promotes CGRP signaling in ILC2s in a cell-intrinsic manner.
- PAC1 deficiency impairs CGRP-mediated inhibition of ILC2 proliferation and IL-13 secretion.
- ILC2-intrinsic PAC1 deficiency worsens Alternaria- or papain-induced allergic airway inflammation in mice.
- In human circulating ILC2s, PAC1 expression negatively correlates with ILC2 abundance and IL13 expression in the reported cohort, and PAC1 influences CGRP-response gene accessibility.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: PAC1 is a source-supported intrinsic regulator that enables CGRP-mediated suppression of ILC2 function in mouse allergic airway models.
- Medium-high confidence: human circulating ILC2 correlations support relevance but are not by themselves causal lung-tissue evidence.
- Low confidence: therapeutic targeting remains a hypothesis, not a clinical recommendation.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse allergic airway inflammation plus human circulating ILC2 association.
- Assay directness: strong mouse perturbation evidence with mechanistic chromatin and signaling readouts.
- Best wiki use: inhibitory neuroimmune regulation, CGRP/PAC1 axis, and ILC2-intrinsic signal tuning.

## Caveats

- Human evidence is circulating ILC2 correlation; do not present it as direct human airway causality.
- CGRP/PAC1 should be kept as inhibitory neuroimmune regulation, distinct from NMU/NMUR1 activation.
