## Why It Matters Here

This source adds a metabolic control node to lung ILC2 activation and connects HIF-1alpha-driven glycolysis to allergic airway inflammation.

## Key Findings

- The source frames lung ILC2 activation in allergic asthma as linked to HIF-1alpha-mediated glycolysis.
- Glycolysis and HIF-1alpha inhibition were used to test mouse and human ILC2 glucose metabolism and function.
- Conditional HIF-1alpha deficiency is used in ILC2-driven airway inflammation models, supporting a more direct ILC2 metabolic mechanism.
- This source strengthens the ILC2 metabolic-regulation branch alongside autophagy, PD-1 metabolism, dopamine/mitochondria, and mTORC1.

## Claim-Level Confidence
