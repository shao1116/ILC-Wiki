## Why It Matters Here

This is an important early mechanistic paper because it shows that ILC2s are not regulated only by alarmins and lipids. They also use costimulatory logic more commonly associated with adaptive lymphocytes, and that logic matters for airway disease outcomes.

## Key Findings

- ILC2s expressed both ICOS and ICOSL, supporting a local costimulatory circuit rather than a purely T-cell-derived interaction.
- Loss of ICOS on murine ILC2s reduced airway hyperreactivity and lung inflammation in the reported airway disease model.
- Blocking ICOS:ICOSL signaling in human ILC2s reduced cytokine production and impaired homeostatic support in the reported assays.
- The source linked ICOS:ICOSL signaling to ILC2 survival and cytokine competence through STAT5-associated signaling.
- Together, the data support ICOS:ICOSL as both a homeostatic and disease-relevant amplifier of ILC2 function.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: ICOS:ICOSL signaling supports ILC2 homeostasis and type 2 effector function in the reported systems.
- High confidence: disrupting this pathway reduces airway-hyperreactivity-associated inflammation in the reported mouse model.
- Medium-high confidence: the source supports an ILC2-centered STAT5-linked survival pathway rather than only a generic association with activation.
- Low confidence: this paper should not be stretched into a universal statement about all costimulatory receptors on ILC2s or all asthma endotypes.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse airway disease plus human ILC2 blockade assays.
- Assay directness: strong for pathway importance in ILC2 survival and cytokine output, strong for airway phenotype in the murine system.
- Best wiki use: ILC2 costimulatory control, homeostatic maintenance, and disease amplification in airway inflammation.

## Caveats

- This is an early mechanistic paper, so later literature adds more complexity to the ILC2 regulatory network.
- The strongest disease claims remain tied to the reported airway model and ex vivo human ILC2 assays.
- ICOS:ICOSL should be interpreted as one major positive regulatory branch, not the only route to ILC2 persistence or activation.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: this source broadens the ILC2 model beyond epithelial alarmins by adding a lymphocyte-like costimulatory layer.
- Supersession status: not superseded; it remains a foundational costimulation paper for ILC2 homeostasis and airway disease.
