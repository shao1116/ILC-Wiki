## Why It Matters Here

This source is important because it shows that not every inflammatory cytokine amplifies ILC2 disease. In early-life rhinovirus-like disease, IL-1beta acts as a brake on ILC2 expansion and type 2 airway pathology.

## Key Findings

- Rhinovirus infection in immature mice induced IL-25, IL-33, type 2 cytokine transcripts, ILC2 expansion, mucus metaplasia, and airway hyperresponsiveness.
- Rhinovirus also induced NLRP3 inflammasome-associated IL-1beta production, with lung macrophages described as a major source.
- Blocking IL-1beta signaling or removing NLRP3 enhanced ILC2/type 2/mucus outcomes in the source text.
- Recombinant IL-1beta logic supports IL-1beta as a negative regulator of this ILC2-driven asthma-like phenotype.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence:
  IL-1beta restrains ILC2 expansion and type 2 airway pathology in this early-life rhinovirus mouse model.
- High confidence:
  the source places NLRP3/IL-1beta signaling as a counter-regulatory branch during virus-induced ILC2 activation.
- Medium confidence:
  the macrophage-derived IL-1beta brake is relevant to lung immune crosstalk, but cell-source details should be checked in full figures for manuscript use.
- Low confidence:
  this source should not be generalized to adult rhinovirus infection or all IL-1beta/ILC2 contexts without additional evidence.

## Methods and Context

- Six-day-old mouse rhinovirus RV-A1B infection model.
- Wild-type and NLRP3-deficient mice.
- IL-1RA, anti-IL-1beta, and recombinant IL-1beta interventions.
- Readouts include ILC2 expansion, type 2 cytokines, mucin-related transcripts, mucus metaplasia, and AHR.

## Caveats

- Early-life mouse model; adult lung and human pediatric asthma require separate evidence.
- IL-1beta can have different effects in other ILC contexts, including ILC3 activation or ILC2 plasticity.
- This source is a negative-regulation branch, not evidence that IL-1beta is broadly anti-inflammatory.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction/tension: IL-1beta restrains ILC2 here, while IL-1beta can activate ILC3/neutrophilic programs in other asthma contexts.
- Supersession status: not superseded; keep as early-life rhinovirus ILC2 brake source.
