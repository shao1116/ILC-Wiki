## Why It Matters Here

This source is useful because it adds a second layer to neuroimmune control: inhibitory signaling can require prior immune-cell priming. It also helps correct the assumption that basophils are only positive amplifiers of type 2 inflammation.

## Key Findings

- In the reported Nippostrongylus brasiliensis lung-inflammation model, basophil depletion exaggerated pulmonary ILC2 responses, eosinophilia, mucus pathology, and lung dysfunction.
- ILC2s from basophil-depleted mice showed reduced expression of the inhibitory neuropeptide receptor NMBR and were less responsive to NMB-mediated suppression.
- Basophils were sufficient to restore or enhance NMBR expression on ILC2s, supporting a direct priming relationship between these cell types.
- The paper argues that this priming does not reduce type 2 responses by eliminating ILC2 activation altogether; instead it equips ILC2s to receive neuron-derived inhibitory signals that help preserve tissue integrity.
- PGE2 contributed to this priming effect in the reported in vitro system, whereas IL-4 alone was not sufficient to recapitulate the full basophil effect.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: in the reported mouse model, basophils limit pulmonary type 2 pathology rather than simply amplifying it.
- High confidence: basophils prime ILC2s for NMB-mediated inhibition by supporting NMBR expression or responsiveness.
- Medium-high confidence: this source supports an accessory-cell gating model in which inhibitory neuroimmune circuits may depend on preceding immune-cell instruction.
- Low confidence: the source should not be generalized to all asthma, all helminth settings, or all basophil biology without preserving the lung-helminth and NMB-specific context.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse helminth-induced pulmonary inflammation with strong lung-functional and pathology readouts.
- Assay directness: strong for basophil dependence and NMBR/NMB-linked regulation in this system; moderate for broader extrapolation to non-helminth airway disease.
- Best wiki use: basophil-ILC2 crosstalk, inhibitory neuroimmune gating, and context-dependent restraint of type 2 inflammation.

## Caveats

- The disease context is helminth-induced pulmonary inflammation, not classical allergen asthma.
- The main mechanistic insight is about inhibitory NMB/NMBR signaling and basophil-dependent priming, so this source should not be used as a universal statement about every basophil-ILC2 interaction.
- Basophils can be pro-inflammatory in other type 2 settings; this paper is valuable precisely because it shows a context-dependent restraint branch.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: this source balances the more familiar literature in which basophils promote ILC2 activation by adding a lung-pathology-limiting branch.
- Supersession status: not superseded; it remains one of the clearest papers for basophil-dependent priming of an inhibitory neuroimmune pathway in ILC2s.
