## Why It Matters Here

This source anchors ILC2 memory-like behavior in allergic lung disease and helps separate memory-like ILC2 amplification from smoke-associated memory-like ILC3 biology.

## Key Findings

- Lung ILC2s proliferated and secreted cytokines after allergen or IL-33 stimulation, followed by a contraction phase.
- Some ILC2s persisted after resolution as allergen-experienced ILC2s.
- Allergen-experienced ILC2s responded more strongly than naive ILC2s to unrelated allergen challenge.
- These cells showed gene-expression features similar to memory T cells and mediated more severe allergic inflammation in the model.
