## Why It Matters Here

This source gives the ILC2 wiki a mechanistically strong metabolism paper rather than a generic "activated cells change metabolism" statement. It is especially useful because it links a defined cell-intrinsic program, autophagy, to survival, cytokine output, and airway physiology in a lung-relevant mouse model.

## Key Findings

- Activated lung ILC2s showed strong use of autophagy in the reported IL-33-driven system.
- Loss of Atg5 in ILC2s reduced IL-5 and IL-13 production, increased apoptosis, and impaired proliferation or homeostatic maintenance after activation.
- Autophagy-deficient ILC2s showed impaired fatty-acid oxidation and TCA/OXPHOS support, increased glycolytic skewing, and metabolic stress consistent with loss of efficient fuel handling.
- In adoptive-transfer experiments, autophagy-deficient ILC2s were less able to drive IL-33-associated airway hyperreactivity and lung inflammation in lymphocyte-deficient recipient mice.
- Gain-of-function autophagy stimulation in the reported system enhanced ILC2 survival and effector output, supporting the idea that autophagy is not just a stress marker but a functional determinant of ILC2 capacity.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: autophagy is required for activated ILC2 survival and type 2 cytokine competence in the reported mouse systems.
- High confidence: impaired autophagy reduces the ability of transferred ILC2s to drive IL-33-associated airway hyperreactivity and lung inflammation.
- Medium-high confidence: the mechanistic bridge between autophagy and ILC2 function runs through altered fatty-acid handling, oxidative metabolism, ROS accumulation, and apoptosis rather than through a single isolated cytokine pathway.
- Low confidence: this source should not be rewritten as proof that autophagy modulation will behave identically in every human asthma endotype or every tissue ILC2 population.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse lung ILC2 biology in IL-33-driven airway inflammation, with ex vivo metabolic assays and adoptive-transfer validation; the paper also references human asthma relevance but the causal data here are mainly murine.
- Assay directness: strong for ILC2-intrinsic metabolic and survival effects, strong for the transfer-linked airway phenotype in the reported model.
- Best wiki use: ILC2 immunometabolism, cell-intrinsic survival biology, and metabolic support of pathogenic type 2 output.

## Caveats

- The disease model is IL-33-driven airway inflammation, which is informative but narrower than the full spectrum of human asthma.
- The strongest claims are cell-intrinsic metabolism and survival claims in mouse ILC2s; translation to human airway ILC2 therapy should remain cautious.
- This source is best used as evidence that metabolic wiring matters for ILC2 effector competence, not as a stand-alone argument for any single drug strategy.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: this source complements other ILC2 metabolism papers by showing that not all metabolic activation is equivalent; glycolytic skewing here accompanies dysfunctional rather than enhanced ILC2 output because the broader autophagy-supported fuel program has collapsed.
- Supersession status: not superseded; it remains one of the clearest source-linked papers for autophagy as a positive regulator of activated ILC2 fitness.
