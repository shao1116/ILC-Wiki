## Key Findings

- 144 volume 13 number 2 february 2012 nature immunology AHR drives the development of gut ILC22 cells and postnatal lymphoid tissues via pathways dependent on and independent of Notch Jacob S Lee1, Marina Cella1, Keely G McDonald2, Cecilia Garlanda3, Gregory D Kennedy4, Manabu Nukaya4, Alberto Mantovani3,5, Raphael Kopan6, Christopher A Bradfield7, Rodney D Newberry2 & Marco Colonna1 Innate lymphoid cells (ILCs) of the ILC22 type protect the intestinal mucosa from infection by secreting interleukin 22 (IL-22).
- ILC22 cells include NKp46+ and lymphoid tissue–inducer (LTi)-like subsets that express the aryl hydrocarbon receptor (AHR).
- Here we found that Ahr–/– mice had a considerable deficit in ILC22 cells that resulted in less secretion of IL-22 and inadequate protection against intestinal bacterial infection.
- AHR induced the transcription factor Notch, which was required for NKp46+ ILCs, whereas LTi-like ILCs, cryptopatches and ILFs were partially dependent on Notch signaling.
- Thus, AHR was essential for ILC22 cells and postnatal intestinal lymphoid tissues.
