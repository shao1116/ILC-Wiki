## Why It Matters Here

This paper is important because it gives the ILC3 wiki one of its most foundational identity anchors. Even though it is gut-centered, later lung and mucosal ILC3 papers repeatedly rely on the idea that AHR is not optional background biology but a core ILC3 state-maintenance axis.

## Key Findings

- Ahr-deficient mice showed a major deficit in gut ILC22 cells together with reduced IL-22 production.
- This deficit impaired protection against intestinal bacterial infection in the reported system.
- AHR induced a Notch-linked developmental program required for NKp46-positive ILCs.
- LTi-like ILCs and postnatal lymphoid tissue structures were also AHR dependent, but their Notch dependence was only partial.
- The source therefore supports AHR as a central developmental and functional regulator across multiple ILC22 or ILC3-like compartments rather than as a minor accessory pathway.
