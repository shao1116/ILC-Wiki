## Key Findings

- The emerging field of innate lymphoid cell (ILC) biol- ogy has progressed rapidly, with many studies suggest- ing important roles for these cells in immunity, tissue development and remodelling1–8.
- ILCs have been cate- gorized into three main groups based on their similarity to T helper (T H) cell subsets9 (FIG.
- In this Review, we do not focus on the biology of lymphoid tissue-inducer (LTi) cells and natural killer (NK) cells or the roles of ILCs in cancer10–14, pregnancy15 or transplantation 16.
- Instead, we focus on the role of ILC1s, ILC2s and ILC3s in autoimmune and inflamma- tory diseases affecting various tissues.
- In particular, we focus on human data and discuss potential therapeutic strategies for targeting ILCs in inflammatory disease.
