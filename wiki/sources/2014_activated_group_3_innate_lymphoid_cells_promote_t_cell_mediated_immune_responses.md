## Why It Matters Here

This source is a useful counterweight to gut ILC3-MHCII tolerance papers. It shows that ILC3 antigen-presentation machinery can support adaptive immune activation in a peripheral lymphoid/splenic setting, whereas gut ILC3 MHCII sources often emphasize restraint of commensal-specific CD4 T cells.

## Key Findings

- IL-1beta stimulation activated fetal-liver-derived and splenic NCR- ILC3s, inducing cytokine secretion, MHCII upregulation, and costimulatory molecules including CD80 and CD86.
- NCR- ILC3s could internalize latex beads, process OVA protein, and stimulate antigen-specific OT-II CD4 T-cell activation in vitro.
- ILC3-specific loss of MHCII reduced OVA-specific CD4 T-cell proliferation in vivo and reduced NP-OVA-specific T-dependent IgG responses after immunization.
- Cognate ILC3-CD4 T-cell interaction was bidirectional: antigen-dependent T-cell contact also activated ILC3s.
- The discussion notes that lamina propria NCR- ILC3s did not show the same costimulatory phenotype, supporting tissue-specific interpretation of ILC3 antigen-presentation outcomes.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: activated peripheral/splenic mouse NCR- ILC3s can promote antigen-specific CD4 T-cell responses in the reported OVA/OT-II systems.
- High confidence: ILC3-intrinsic MHCII contributes to T-dependent B-cell antibody responses in this immunization setting.
- Medium-high confidence: the source supports context-dependent ILC3 antigen presentation as either immune-activating or tolerogenic depending on tissue and inflammatory cues.
- Low confidence: this source does not establish direct lung ILC3 regulation of T-cell or B-cell immunity.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse peripheral lymphoid/splenic ILC3 systems, with fetal-liver-derived NCR- ILC3s and ex vivo splenic NCR- ILC3s.
- Main assays: RORgammat-Cre ILC3 MHCII deletion, OT-II adoptive transfer, OVA/NP-OVA immunization, antibody titers, flow cytometry, bead uptake, IL-1beta/TLR stimulation, and ILC3-T-cell coculture.
- Assay directness: strong for adaptive immune activation by peripheral/splenic ILC3s; indirect for lung disease.
- Best wiki use: adaptive-immunity topic, ILC3 antigen-presentation context, and contrast with gut ILC3 tolerance mechanisms.

## Caveats

- Keep this source separate from gut ILC3-MHCII tolerance papers; the output here is immune activation in peripheral/splenic antigen-presentation settings.
- Do not generalize the result to all ILC3s or all tissues; lamina propria ILC3s are explicitly discussed as different.
- Do not promote to lung causality without pulmonary ILC3 antigen-presentation evidence.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: explains why ILC3 antigen presentation can be activating in one context and tolerogenic in another.
- Supersession status: complements, rather than supersedes, gut ILC3 MHCII tolerance sources.
