## Why It Matters Here

This source makes the ILC2-eosinophil axis bidirectional: ILC2s promote eosinophilia, but eosinophils can also support ILC2 effector function.

## Key Findings

- The source tests whether eosinophils regulate ILC2 responses in allergic asthma and IL-33-driven type 2 airway inflammation.
- The paper supports eosinophils as positive regulators of lung ILC2 effector function in mouse allergic airway inflammation.
- This adds a feed-forward cellular circuit to the ILC2 disease model rather than a single cytokine-only axis.
