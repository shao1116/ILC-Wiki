## Why It Matters Here

This source adds a restraint branch to the ILC2 asthma map: severe asthma models can be attenuated by hUC-MSC treatment, with ILC2 and Th2 cytokine production treated as direct immune targets rather than only downstream markers.

## Key Findings

- hUC-MSC treatment attenuated lung type 2 inflammation in Alternaria- and HDM/DEP-induced severe asthma models.
- The source reports reduced IL-5 and IL-13 production from Th2 cells and ILC2s as part of the anti-inflammatory effect.
- In vitro experiments support direct suppression of asthmogenic cytokine production from differentiated mouse T cells or PBMCs from asthma patients.
- The paper is strongest as a mechanistic restraint source, not as a clinical recommendation for MSC therapy.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: hUC-MSC treatment suppresses lung type 2 inflammation in the reported murine severe-asthma models.
- Medium-high confidence: MSCs can directly suppress IL-5/IL-13-producing Th2 and ILC2 responses under the reported in vitro/ex vivo conditions.
- Low confidence: this source alone should not be used to infer clinical efficacy, dosing, or safety of MSC therapy in human severe asthma.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse severe asthma models plus human asthma PBMC-related assays.
- Assay directness: strong for mouse lung inflammatory readouts; supportive for direct human immune-cell cytokine suppression.
- Best wiki use: ILC2 regulation, type 2 asthma restraint, and cell-therapy-adjacent mechanistic context.

## Caveats

- MSC effects are pleiotropic; do not frame them as ILC2-exclusive.
- Human evidence in this source is ex vivo/in vitro rather than a therapeutic trial endpoint.
- Keep Alternaria and HDM/DEP model labels visible when discussing disease relevance.
