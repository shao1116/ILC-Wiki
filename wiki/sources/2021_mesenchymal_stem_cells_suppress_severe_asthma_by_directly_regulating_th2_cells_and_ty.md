## Key Findings

- Patients with severe asthma have unmet clinical needs for effective and safe therapies.
- One possibility may be mesenchymal stem cell (MSC) therapy, which can improve asthma in murine models.
- However, it remains unclear how MSCs exert their beneficial effects in asthma.
- Here, we examined the effect of human umbilical cord blood-derived MSCs (hUC-MSC) on two mouse models of severe asthma, namely, Alternaria alternata-induced and house dust mite (HDM)/diesel exhaust particle (DEP)-induced asthma. hUC- MSC treatment attenuated lung type 2 (Th2 and type 2 innate lymphoid cell) inflammation in both models.
- In vitro co-culture showed that hUC- MSC directly downregulated the interleukin (IL)-5 and IL-13 production of differentiated mouse Th2 cells and peripheral blood mononuclear cells from asthma patients.
