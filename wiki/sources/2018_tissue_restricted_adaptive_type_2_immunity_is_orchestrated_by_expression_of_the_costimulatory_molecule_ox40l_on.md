- Primary biological axis: IL-33 induces ILC2 OX40L, which co-stimulates local adaptive type 2 immunity in lung and adipose tissue.

## Why It Matters Here

This paper gives the ILC2 regulation map a concrete adaptive-immune crosstalk mechanism: ILC2s do not only secrete type 2 cytokines, but can license local Th2 and Treg expansion through OX40L.

## Key Findings

- IL-33 administration induced organ-specific surface OX40L expression on ILC2s.
- ILC2 OX40L supported local Th2 and Treg expansion in lung and adipose tissue.
- ILC2-targeted OX40L loss impaired adaptive type 2 pulmonary inflammation after Nippostrongylus brasiliensis infection or allergen exposure.
- The source is strongest for mouse tissue-restricted costimulation, not for direct human asthma therapeutic prediction.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: IL-33-activated ILC2s can use OX40L to orchestrate local adaptive type 2 responses in the reported mouse systems.
- Medium-high confidence: OX40L belongs in the ILC2 costimulatory/crosstalk regulatory layer.
- Low confidence: this source alone should not be used to infer human anti-OX40L efficacy in asthma.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse IL-33, helminth, and allergen-exposure systems.
- Compartment: lung and adipose tissue.
- Assay directness: strong for genetic perturbation of OX40L-dependent tissue type 2 responses.
- Best wiki use: ILC2-T cell crosstalk and costimulatory regulation.

## Caveats

- Keep Th2, Treg, and ILC2 contributions separated.
- Treat OX40L as a costimulatory axis, not as a cytokine-output substitute.
- Human relevance remains translational unless matched human airway data are cited.
