## Why It Matters Here

This source adds a clinically oriented inhibitory counterpart to the PGD2/lipid-activating ILC2 literature. It helps the wiki distinguish between evidence that PGD2 activates ILC2s and evidence that pharmacologic blockade of that axis can directly suppress several human ILC2 effector behaviors.

## Key Findings

- Fevipiprant inhibited PGD2-induced migration of human ILC2s with nanomolar potency in the reported assays.
- Fevipiprant reduced PGD2-supported survival and blocked PGD2-induced type 2 cytokine production by human ILC2s.
- PGD2 induced ILC2 aggregation and upregulated adhesion-associated molecules including ICAM1 and PECAM1, and fevipiprant blocked these effects.
- Inhibitory effects were observed across healthy and asthmatic donor-derived ILC2 preparations in the reported ex vivo system.
- The source supports DP2 as a tractable upstream node for suppressing human ILC2 activation rather than only neutralizing downstream cytokines.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: fevipiprant blocks PGD2/DP2-driven migration, aggregation, and cytokine production in human ILC2s in this source.
- High confidence: PGD2-driven adhesion and aggregation are part of the ILC2 functional program inhibited by fevipiprant here.
- Medium-high confidence: these findings support translational relevance for eosinophilic asthma biology, but the source is still mainly mechanistic and ex vivo at the ILC2 level.
- Low confidence: this source should not be used alone to claim clinical benefit in all asthma endotypes or to define in vivo lung-tissue ILC2 behavior.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: human blood-derived ILC2s, including cells from donors with asthma.
- Assay directness: strong for DP2 pharmacology at the human ILC2 level; limited for direct tissue-level causality.
- Best wiki use: human inhibitory lipid axis, translational comparator for PGD2 biology, and mechanistic support for upstream ILC2 targeting.

## Caveats

- Most direct evidence is ex vivo rather than from lung tissue or in vivo airway sampling.
- Asthma donor material strengthens disease relevance but does not replace direct airway-tissue measurements.
- Drug-development framing should remain source-linked and not be generalized into blanket therapeutic claims.
