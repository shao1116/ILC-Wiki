## Why It Matters Here

This source extends lung ILC2 biology beyond asthma and repair into tumor-niche immunoregulation, where ILC2-driven type 2 inflammation can suppress NK-mediated antimetastatic immunity.

## Key Findings

- The source text states that IL-33-dependent activation of lung-resident ILC2s promotes lung metastatic seeding and mortality in multiple models.
- Activated lung ILC2s suppress NK cell-driven innate antitumor immunity in the premetastatic lung niche.
- The paper supports an innate checkpoint-style model in which type 2 lung inflammation can antagonize type 1/NK antitumor defense.
- This source should be separated from asthma ILC2 biology because the disease endpoint is lung metastasis.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: lung ILC2 activation can suppress NK antimetastatic function in the reported mouse lung metastasis systems.

- Low confidence: this source does not imply that all ILC2 activation is tumor-promoting or that human lung metastasis behaves identically.
