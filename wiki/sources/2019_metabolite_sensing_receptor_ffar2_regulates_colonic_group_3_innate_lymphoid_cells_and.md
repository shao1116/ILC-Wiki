## Key Findings

- Colonic ILC3s expressed FFAR2, a microbial-metabolite-sensing receptor.
- FFAR2 agonism promoted ILC3 expansion and function.
- ILC3 FFAR2 deficiency decreased proliferation and IL-22 production, with impaired epithelial defense and increased injury/infection susceptibility.
- This is gut metabolite-sensing evidence, not direct lung ILC3 metabolism evidence.
