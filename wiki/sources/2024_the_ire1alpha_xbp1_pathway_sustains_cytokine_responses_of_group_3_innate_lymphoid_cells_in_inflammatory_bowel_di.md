## Why It Matters Here

This source gives the ILC3 wiki a strong stress-response pathway that is more specific than a generic "activated cells experience ER stress" statement. It is also unusually useful because it combines mouse mechanism, human pharmacology, and a potential clinical biomarker angle.

## Key Findings

- IL-23 and IL-1beta selectively activated the IRE1alpha/XBP1 branch of the unfolded-protein response in intestinal ILC3s through mitochondrial ROS.
- Ire1alpha deletion in Rorc-expressing cells reduced ILC3 expression of ER-stress and cytokine genes, including Il22 and Il17a, in the reported models.
- Mice lacking this pathway in ILC3-associated compartments were more susceptible to C. difficile infection and to DSS- or T-cell-transfer-driven colitis; exogenous IL-22 partly rescued the DSS phenotype.
- In human intestinal ILC3s, selective IRE1 inhibitors reduced IL-22 output, whereas an IRE1 activator increased IL-22 production under low-dose IL-23 stimulation.
- Pretreatment frequencies of XBP1s-positive ILC3s in Crohn's disease correlated with response to ustekinumab, supporting potential biomarker relevance within IBD.

## Caveats

- The source is focused on intestinal inflammation and IBD.
- Rorc-Cre-based interpretation should stay aware that not all effects in vivo are uniquely attributable to ILC3s unless the paper's supporting controls are kept in mind.
- This source is best used to define a strong extrapulmonary mechanism candidate rather than direct lung-disease proof.
