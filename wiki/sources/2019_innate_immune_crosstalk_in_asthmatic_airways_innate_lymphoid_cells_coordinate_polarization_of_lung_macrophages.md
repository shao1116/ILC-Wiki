## Why It Matters Here

This source connects human airway ILC subset balance to macrophage polarization and asthma phenotype. It complements smoke-associated ILC3/M1 findings and helps interpret ILC2 versus ILC1/3 branches in asthma.

## Key Findings

- ILC1s and ILC3s, in addition to ILC2s, were increased in induced sputum from asthma patients relative to healthy donors.
- ILC2 numbers correlated with M2 macrophages, while ILC1/ILC3 numbers correlated with M1 macrophages.
- Coculture of ILC2s with alveolar macrophages induced M2-related genes, whereas ILC1/ILC3 coculture induced M1-related genes through cytokine secretion and cell-cell contact.
- Eosinophilic asthma showed more ILC2/M2 profile, whereas noneosinophilic asthma showed an M1 macrophage-dominant profile.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence:
  airway ILC subset composition differs by asthma phenotype in this human induced-sputum study.
- High confidence:
  ILC2s align with M2-like macrophage polarization, while ILC1/ILC3s align with M1-like macrophage polarization in the reported assays.
- Medium confidence:
  ILC-macrophage crosstalk may help structure eosinophilic versus noneosinophilic asthma, but patient-level causality remains incompletely proven.
- Low confidence:
  induced sputum findings should not be treated as a full map of lung tissue-resident ILC niches.

## Methods and Context

- Induced sputum from steroid-naive asthma subjects and healthy donors.
- Flow cytometry of ILC subsets and macrophage phenotypes.
- Sorted alveolar macrophage and ILC subset coculture.
- Best used for airway immune-crosstalk and phenotype association.

## Caveats

- Sputum cell composition differs from parenchymal lung tissue.
- Coculture data support functional capacity but do not fully reconstruct the in vivo airway niche.
- Steroid-naive cohort context should be kept separate from steroid-resistant asthma claims.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction/tension: ILC2-M2 and ILC1/3-M1 branches should be treated as phenotype-linked patterns, not universal asthma rules.
- Supersession status: not superseded; use as a human airway crosstalk source.
