## Why It Matters Here

This source anchors the stromal ILC3 branch for neutrophilic asthma and provides a direct counterpart to ILC2 stromal/fibroblast regulation.

## Key Findings

- SCF expression was increased and positively correlated with IL-17A and MPO expression in asthmatic patients in the source text.
- ILC3s were identified as major IL-17A-producing responders to SCF in the lung.
- SCF synergized with IL-1beta/IL-23 to enhance pulmonary ILC3 activation and neutrophilic inflammation in mice.
- c-Kit deficiency, fibroblast-specific SCF deletion, and imatinib treatment reduced ILC3 proliferation/IL-17A output, AHR, or neutrophilic inflammation in the reported systems.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: fibroblast-derived SCF/KIT augments pulmonary ILC3 IL-17A and neutrophilic asthma-like inflammation in this source.
- Medium-high confidence: human correlations support disease relevance, but human causality remains inferential.
- Low confidence: imatinib remains proof-of-concept pathway inhibition here, not a clinical recommendation.

## Methods and Context

- Human asthma expression/correlation analysis involving SCF, IL-17A, and MPO.
- Mouse neutrophilic asthma models involving LPS, air pollution, and OVA/LPS contexts as described in source text.
- SCF/c-Kit perturbation including c-Kit deficiency and fibroblast-specific SCF deletion.
- Transcriptomic/signaling analysis implicating AKT and STAT3.
- Imatinib used as c-Kit inhibitor intervention in experimental models.
