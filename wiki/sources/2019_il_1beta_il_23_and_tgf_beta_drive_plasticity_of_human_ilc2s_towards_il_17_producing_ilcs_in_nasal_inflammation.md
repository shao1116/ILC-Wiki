## Why It Matters Here

This source is not a direct lung parenchyma paper, but it is high-value for human airway ILC plasticity. It supports a cautious human correlate for IL-17-producing ILC2-like states seen in mouse lung inflammation.

## Key Findings

- Human ILC2s transdifferentiated into IL-17-secreting cells in the presence of IL-1beta, IL-23, and TGF-beta.
- IL-4 or vitamin D3 abrogated this conversion in the reported system.
- IL-17-producing ILC2-derived cells induced IL-8 secretion by epithelial cells.
- Their presence in cystic-fibrosis nasal polyps was associated with neutrophilia.

## Methods and Context

- Human nasal polyp and cystic fibrosis nasal inflammation context.

## Caveats

- Nasal polyp and cystic fibrosis contexts are not equivalent to lower airway asthma, COPD, or lung infection.
- Use as human mucosal plasticity evidence with tissue label.
- Association with neutrophilia should not be overstated as causality in lung disease.
