## Why It Matters Here

This source adds a granulocyte-derived lipid-mediator input to ILC3 regulation. It is valuable for the ILC3 mechanism map because it connects eosinophils to IL-22-producing colonic ILC3s through PGE2/EP4, but it should remain gut-labeled and should not be used as direct lung ILC3 evidence.

## Key Findings

- Colonic eosinophils upregulated Ptgs2/COX-2 during DSS colitis and were a major Ptgs2-expressing population in inflamed colon tissue.
- Eosinophil-specific Ptgs2 deletion worsened DSS and TNBS colitis, reduced colonic PGE2 and IL-22, and reduced epithelial STAT3 activation.
- Recombinant IL-22 and a stable PGE2 analog rescued key protective readouts in the reported mouse colitis systems.
- CCR6+ colonic ILC3s were the main IL-22-producing ILC3 subset in the reported flow analyses; Ptgs2 deletion reduced IL-22+ ILC3 frequency without markedly changing total ILC3 abundance.
- EP4 antagonism blocked PGE2-enhanced IL-22 production in colon culture or purified ILC3 assays, supporting a direct EP4-dependent ILC3 response.
- DSS injury increased eosinophil proximity to RORgammat+ ILC3s, providing spatial support for the functional interaction.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: eosinophil-derived COX-2/PGE2 supports colonic ILC3 IL-22 production in the reported mouse colitis models.
- High confidence: PGE2 acts through an EP4-dependent route to enhance ILC3 IL-22 output in the reported assays.
- Medium-high confidence: this source supports a broader principle that non-ILC immune cells can tune ILC3 barrier-protective output through lipid mediators.
- Low confidence: this source does not establish lung eosinophil-to-ILC3 regulation or pulmonary therapeutic relevance.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse DSS and TNBS experimental colitis, with human IBD discussed as context rather than direct perturbation evidence.
- Main assays: eosinophil-specific Ptgs2 deletion, bulk RNA-seq/qPCR, RNAscope/immunofluorescence, LC/MS and ELISA for prostaglandins, recombinant IL-22 rescue, PGE2 analog treatment, EP receptor antagonism, flow cytometry for ILC3 subsets, purified ILC3 stimulation, and spatial proximity analysis.
- Assay directness: strong for gut ILC3 IL-22 regulation; indirect for lung ILC3 biology.
- Best wiki use: ILC3 functional regulation, gut IL-22/repair context, eosinophil-ILC3 crosstalk, and lipid mediator guardrails.

## Caveats

- Keep the DSS/TNBS colitis and gut tissue labels visible.
- The study does not show that eosinophil COX-2 is universally protective in all IBD settings or all tissues.
- The human disease discussion supports relevance, but the mechanistic perturbation is mouse colitis.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: helps explain why eosinophils can be pathogenic or protective depending on mediator output and disease context.
- Supersession status: adds a PGE2/EP4 lipid-mediator branch to ILC3 IL-22 regulation without replacing AHR, FFAR2, VIP, LINGO4, BACH2, or other gut ILC3 pathways.
