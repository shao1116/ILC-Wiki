## Key Findings

- TLR9 activation suppresses IL-33-mediated airway hyperreactivity and airway inflammation through inhibition of ILC2s.
- The suppressive pathway involves type I interferon-driven NK-cell IFN-gamma production and direct IFN-gamma/STAT1 signaling in ILC2s.
- MIS416 suppresses Alternaria-induced allergic airway inflammation and inhibits human ILC2 expansion/function in humanized NSG or ex vivo-linked systems described by the source.
- This paper supports IFN-gamma as a therapeutically interesting but context-dependent brake on ILC2-driven type 2 airway disease.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: TLR9 activation can inhibit ILC2-driven AHR in the reported mouse allergic-airway systems.
- High confidence: IFN-gamma and STAT1 are source-supported direct inhibitory nodes for ILC2 function.
- Medium confidence: MIS416 and TLR9 agonism remain preclinical/translational hypotheses, not clinical recommendations for asthma.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mouse IL-33 and Alternaria airway inflammation plus humanized/ex vivo ILC2 evidence.
- Assay directness: strong perturbation evidence for TLR9/interferon/STAT1-mediated ILC2 inhibition.
- Best wiki use: microbial-sensing brake, IFN-gamma/STAT1 ILC2 suppression, allergic AHR regulation.

## Caveats

- Separate TLR9 agonist effects on the whole immune system from ILC2-intrinsic IFN-gamma/STAT1 signaling.
- Do not present MIS416 as established asthma therapy.
