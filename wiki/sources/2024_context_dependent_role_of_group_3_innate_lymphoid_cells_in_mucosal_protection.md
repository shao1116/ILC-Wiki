## Key Findings

- Ivanov1,5* How group 3 innate lymphoid cells (ILC3s) regulate mucosal protection in the presence of T cells remains poorly understood.
- Here, we examined ILC3 function in intestinal immunity using ILC3- deficient mice that maintain en- dogenous T cells, T helper 17 (TH17) cells, and secondary lymphoid organs.
- ILC3s were dispensable for generation of TH17 and TH22 cell responses to commensal and pathogenic bacteria, and absence of ILC3s did not affect IL- 22 production by CD4 T cells before or during infection.
- However, despite the presence of IL- 22–producing T cells, ILC3s and ILC3- derived IL- 22 were required for maintaining homeostatic functions of the intestinal epithelium.
- T cell–sufficient, ILC3- deficient mice were capable of pathogen clearance and survived infection with a low dose of Citrobacter rodentium.
