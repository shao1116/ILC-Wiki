## Why It Matters Here

This is the key smoke-asthma ILC3 source in the wiki. It connects smoking, sputum ILC3, CD45RO+ memory-like ILC3s, neutrophil-rich inflammation, M1 macrophage association, and epithelial IL-1beta.

## Key Findings

- Smoking asthma subjects had higher frequencies of pro-inflammatory NCR- ILC3s in induced sputum than non-smoking asthma subjects.
- Smoking was associated with CD45RO-expressing memory-like ILC3s in blood.
- ILC3 frequencies correlated with smoking amount, disease severity, circulating neutrophils, and M1 alveolar macrophage frequencies, but not eosinophil frequencies.
- Cigarette smoke extract induced CD45RO expression in ILC3s in vitro and impaired airway epithelial barrier function while increasing epithelial IL-1beta production.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence:
  cigarette smoking in asthma is associated with increased sputum NCR- ILC3s and blood CD45RO+ memory-like ILC3s in this cohort.
- High confidence:
  the ILC3 signal aligns more with neutrophil/M1 macrophage-associated non-allergic inflammation than with eosinophilic allergy in this source.
- Medium-high confidence:
  cigarette smoke extract can promote CD45RO expression in ILC3s and epithelial IL-1beta-associated activation signals in vitro.
- Low confidence:
  the source does not by itself prove that ILC3s are the sole causal driver of smoking-aggravated asthma severity in patients.

## Methods and Context

- Paired sputum and blood samples from smoking and non-smoking asthma subjects, plus healthy smoker/non-smoker comparison groups.
- ILC subset flow cytometry; ILC3s include NCR- and CD45RO+ memory-like compartments.
- In vitro cigarette smoke extract experiments with ILCs and airway epithelial cells.
- Best used for human association plus ex vivo/in vitro mechanism, not for definitive clinical intervention claims.

## Caveats

- Clinical associations do not prove direction of causality.
- CD45RO is interpreted as memory-like/activation-associated in ILCs; its exact functional meaning is less established than in T cells.
- Sputum and blood ILC3 compartments should not automatically be treated as identical to resident lung tissue ILC3s.
