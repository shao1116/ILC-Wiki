## Why It Matters Here

This source makes PD-1 a metabolic brake on ILC2 activation, creating a checkpoint branch distinct from PD-1 blockade in cancer.

## Key Findings

- PD-1 limits ILC2 viability and downregulates effector function during IL-33-induced airway inflammation.
- PD-1 deficiency shifts ILC2 metabolism toward glycolysis, glutaminolysis, and methionine catabolism.
- PD-1 blockade worsens AHR in the reported system, while a PD-1 agonist ameliorates AHR and lung inflammation in a humanized mouse model.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: PD-1 acts as a metabolic checkpoint limiting pulmonary ILC2 activation in the reported models.
- Medium-high confidence: PD-1 agonism is a mechanistically coherent preclinical intervention, but not a clinical recommendation.
- Low confidence: do not assume PD-1 pathway manipulation has the same effect across cancer and asthma contexts.
