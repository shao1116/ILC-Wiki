## Key Findings

- Karagiannis and colleagues reveal that external lipids transiently stored in lipid droplets are required to fuel pathogenic ILC2 responses and identify a ketogenic diet as a potent intervention strategy to treat ILC2-driven airway inﬂammation.
- However, chronic activation of ILCs results in im- mune-mediated pathology.
- Here, we show that tis- sue-resident type 2 ILCs (ILC2s) display a distinct metabolic signature upon chronic activation.
- In the context of allergen-driven airway inﬂammation, ILC2s increase their uptake of both external lipids and glucose.
- Externally acquired fatty acids are tran- siently stored in lipid droplets and converted into phospholipids to promote the proliferation of ILC2s.
