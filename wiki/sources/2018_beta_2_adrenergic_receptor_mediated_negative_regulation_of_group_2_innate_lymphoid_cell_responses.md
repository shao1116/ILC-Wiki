## Why It Matters Here

This source is one of the clearest papers showing that neuroimmune input can directly restrain, not just activate, ILC2 biology. It is valuable for the lung wiki because it prevents the neuroimmune layer from being written as uniformly pro-inflammatory.

## Key Findings

- Murine ILC2s expressed beta2-adrenergic receptor, placing them in direct communication with adrenergic neuroimmune signals.
- Genetic loss of beta2AR exaggerated ILC2 responses and type 2 inflammation in the reported systems, including lung inflammatory outcomes.
- Pharmacologic beta2AR agonism suppressed ILC2 responses and reduced type 2 inflammation in vivo.
- The source argues that the inhibitory effect is cell-intrinsic to ILC2s and operates through reduced proliferation and effector function rather than only through a secondary stromal or myeloid pathway.
- The study therefore supports adrenergic input as an authentic restraint branch within ILC2 biology.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: beta2-adrenergic signaling restrains ILC2 proliferation and effector function in the reported mouse models.
- High confidence: loss of this pathway exaggerates type 2 inflammatory outcomes, including lung inflammation.
- Medium-high confidence: the paper supports a direct neuroimmune brake on ILC2s rather than merely an indirect change in surrounding tissue tone.
- Low confidence: this source should not be stretched into a clinical claim about every beta-agonist exposure in human asthma without matched human ILC2 evidence and therapeutic context.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: mainly mouse neuroimmune biology with intestine-centered localization detail and both gut and lung inflammatory phenotypes.
- Assay directness: strong for receptor expression, perturbation, and inhibitory effect on ILC2 responses; moderate for tissue-specific generalization across organs.
- Best wiki use: neuroimmune restraint, adrenergic inhibition, and counterbalance to NMU- or other excitatory ILC2 pathways.

## Caveats

- Some spatial or anatomic observations in this source are intestine-centered, so lung interpretation should emphasize the conserved inhibitory pathway rather than assume identical nerve-ILC2 geometry in every organ.
- The strongest causal data are preclinical and mouse-based.
- This source is best used to establish the existence of an adrenergic brake, not to resolve all therapeutic adrenergic questions in human airway disease.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: this paper productively contradicts any oversimplified view that neuroimmune signaling only amplifies ILC2 responses.
- Supersession status: not superseded; it remains a core inhibitory-neuroimmune reference for the ILC2 mechanism map.
