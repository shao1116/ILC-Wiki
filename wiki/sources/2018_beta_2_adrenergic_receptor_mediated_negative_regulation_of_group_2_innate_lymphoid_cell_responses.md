## Key Findings

- NEUROIMMUNOLOGY b2-adrenergic receptor–mediated negative regulation of group 2 innate lymphoid cell responses Saya Moriyama,1 Jonathan R.
- Although recent studies identified group 2 innate lymphoid cells (ILC2s) as potent sources of type 2 cytokines, the molecular pathways controlling ILC2 responses are incompletely defined.
- Here we demonstrate that murine ILC2s express the b 2-adrenergic receptor ( b2AR) and colocalize with adrenergic neurons in the intestine. b2AR deficiency resulted in exaggerated ILC2 responses and type 2 inflammation in intestinal and lung tissues.
- Conversely,b 2AR agonist treatment was associated with impaired ILC2 responses and reduced inflammation in vivo.
- Mechanistically, we demonstrate that theb2AR pathway is a cell-intrinsic negative regulator of ILC2 responses through inhibition of cell proliferation and effector function.
