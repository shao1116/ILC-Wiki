## Why It Matters Here

## Key Findings

- IL-33-activated ILC2s were reported as crucial for AHR and airway inflammation during RSV infection.
- ILC2-derived IL-13 was sufficient for RSV-driven AHR in the reported reconstitution system.
- Peripheral eosinophilia is linked to both ILC2 and myeloid IL-33 signaling.
- This source supports a viral IL-33-ILC2-IL-13 AHR branch distinct from post-viral repair programs.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: pulmonary IL-33-activated ILC2s mediate RSV-triggered AHR and airway inflammation in the reported mouse system.

- Low confidence: it should not be collapsed with influenza repair biology or all pediatric RSV-asthma causality.
