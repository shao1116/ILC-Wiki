## Why It Matters Here

This is a core lung ILC3 host-defense paper. It supports the protective/barrier-defense branch of ILC3 biology in pulmonary bacterial infection and should be kept distinct from IL-17-driven inflammatory ILC3 disease models.

## Key Findings

- Mouse lung mucosa contained a discrete ILC3 population expressing CD127, CD90, CCR6, and RORgammat.
- Lung ILC3s were a major source of IL-22 after IL-23 stimulation.
- During Streptococcus pneumoniae infection, ILC3s rapidly accumulated in lung tissue and produced IL-22.
- Dendritic cells and MyD88-dependent innate signaling were implicated in IL-22 production by ILC3s.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence:
  lung ILC3s can produce IL-22 during Streptococcus pneumoniae infection in this mouse model.
- High confidence:

- Medium confidence:
  the dendritic cell/MyD88 upstream axis is source-supported, but the exact generality across pathogens should be tested separately.
