## Evidence Profile

- Directness to this project: very high for lung ILC2/ILC3-boundary plasticity and IL-17-linked pathology.

- Main biological axis: papain/IL-33 lung inflammation + leukotriene signals -> Ahr-facilitated ST2+ ILC2 IL-17 production -> more pathogenic lung inflammation.

## Why It Matters Here

This source is a major caution against treating ILC2 as purely type 2. It gives a lung-specific IL-17-producing ST2+ ILC2 branch that intersects with ILC3-like inflammatory interpretation.

## Key Findings

- Papain or IL-33 treatment boosted IL-17 production from ST2+ ILC2s, termed ILC217s in the source.
- Ahr facilitated IL-17 production by ILC217s, whereas RORgammat was not the central requirement described in the abstract text.
- IL-33 and leukotrienes synergized to promote IL-17 production in ILC2s through NFAT-linked signaling.
- Il17-deficient ILC2s were less pathogenic after transfer in lung inflammation models.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence:
  ST2+ lung ILC2s can produce IL-17 in papain or IL-33-driven lung inflammation models.
- High confidence:
  IL-33 and leukotrienes synergize to promote pathogenic IL-17-producing ILC2s in this source.
- Medium-high confidence:
  Ahr is an important regulatory node for this ILC2 IL-17 program in the reported model.
- Low confidence:
  these cells should not be automatically relabeled as conventional ILC3s without marker and lineage context.

## Methods and Context

- Mouse papain and IL-33 lung inflammation models.
- Flow cytometry of lung ILC2 IL-17 production.
- Ahr-deficient and Il17-deficient transfer contexts.
- Best used for plastic ILC2/IL-17 pathology, not for canonical ILC3 identity.

## Caveats

- The source's IL-17-producing ILC2s are ST2+ ILC2-like cells, not automatically stable ILC3s.
- Mouse lung inflammation context should be separated from human asthma claims.
- IL-17 output coexists with IL-5/IL-13 output in the source, reinforcing mixed effector state interpretation.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction/tension: creates a boundary-state branch between ILC2 and ILC3 inflammatory programs.
- Supersession status: not superseded; use to qualify simple type 2-only ILC2 statements.
