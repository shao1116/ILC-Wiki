## Why It Matters Here

This source gives the ILC3 wiki a clean negative-regulation branch in human cells. That is valuable because many ILC3 mechanism pages otherwise lean heavily toward activation biology.

## Key Findings

- The paper examined how human mucosal ILC3s respond to IL-23 plus IL-1beta stimulation in the presence of active vitamin D.
- Vitamin D downregulated the IL-23 receptor pathway in these cells.
- The paper therefore supports vitamin D as a brake on inflammatory ILC3 responsiveness rather than as a generic background association.
- Because the cells were human and mucosal, the source is especially useful for translational mechanism context even though it is not a lung paper.
- This source is best used to support a restraint branch opposite IL-23-driven activation biology.
