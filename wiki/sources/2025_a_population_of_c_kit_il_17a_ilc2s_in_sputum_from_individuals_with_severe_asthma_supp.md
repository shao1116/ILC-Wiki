## Why It Matters Here

This paper gives the wiki a direct human sputum anchor for the ILC2-to-ILC3-like boundary problem in severe asthma. It is especially useful because it separates eosinophilic, neutrophilic, and mixed granulocytic airway inflammation rather than treating severe asthma as one immune state.

## Key Findings

- Sputum ILC3s were increased in severe asthma with intense airway neutrophilia.
- Mixed granulocytic severe asthma showed raised sputum ILC2s and ILC3s and contained an intermediate ILC2 population with CRTH2, IL-5/IL-13, c-kit, and IL-17A features.
- Intermediate ILC2s correlated with airway neutrophilia and were associated with increased IL-1beta and IL-18 in sputum supernatants.
- Sort-purified canonical human ILC2s cultured with IL-1beta plus IL-18 upregulated c-kit and IL-17A and acquired transcriptomic features related to both type 2 and type 17 inflammatory programs.
- The source supports a human airway compartment claim, not a direct lower-lung tissue lineage-tracing claim.

## Claim-Level Confidence

- High confidence: in this severe-asthma sputum cohort, ILC3s associate with neutrophilic airway inflammation and IL-17A+ c-kit+ intermediate ILC2s are enriched in mixed granulocytic inflammation.
- High confidence: IL-1beta plus IL-18 can induce c-kit and IL-17A in sort-purified human ILC2s under the reported in vitro conditions.
- Medium-high confidence: the paper supports ILC2-to-ILC3-like plasticity as a human airway working model, but it does not prove irreversible lineage conversion in vivo.
- Low confidence: this source should not be used to claim that all IL-17+ ILCs in asthma are ILC2-derived or that sputum states fully represent lung parenchymal ILC biology.

## Methods and Context

- Species/context: human severe asthma, mild asthma comparators, and in vitro human ILC2 culture.
- Compartment: induced sputum and peripheral-blood-derived sorted ILC2s for culture experiments; this is airway evidence, not direct lung tissue histology.
- Assay directness: strong for flow-cytometric abundance and phenotype; supportive for cytokine-associated plasticity; limited for in vivo lineage direction.
- Best wiki use: severe-asthma ILC2/ILC3 boundary states, neutrophilic and mixed granulocytic airway inflammation, and IL-1beta/IL-18-associated ILC2 plasticity.

## Caveats

- Preserve the sputum/airway compartment label when reusing this source.
- The title uses "trans-differentiation," but the safest wiki wording is "ILC2-to-ILC3-like boundary state" unless the claim is specifically about the authors' in vitro induction experiments.
- Human cohort association and in vitro cytokine stimulation are complementary but not the same as in vivo fate mapping.
- This source should refine, not erase, earlier nasal-polyp and mouse lung IL-17-producing ILC2/ILC3-like literature.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction status: complements earlier nasal inflammation and c-kit+ ILC2 literature by adding human sputum severe-asthma evidence.
- Supersession status: partially supersedes purely review-level statements about severe-asthma ILC2/ILC3 plasticity, but does not supersede mouse perturbation studies or lower-lung tissue data.
