## Why It Matters Here

This source upgrades ILC2 from an effector population to a niche instructor for macrophage identity. It is especially useful for connecting lung ILC biology to monocyte-derived alveolar macrophage programs after viral exposure.

## Key Findings

- After MuHV-4 infection, lung-resident and recruited ILC2s had reduced expansion and type 2 cytokine production after HDM challenge.
- MuHV-4 infection triggered GM-CSF production by lung ILC2s.
- ILC2-derived GM-CSF instructed monocyte differentiation into alveolar macrophages without promoting type 2 macrophage functions.
- Virus-conditioned ILC2s shaped pulmonary niche identity beyond the initial acute infection and contributed to protection against allergic airway disease.

## Methods and Context

- Mouse murid herpesvirus 4 infection followed by house dust mite airway challenge.
- Analysis of lung-resident and recruited ILC2s.
- GM-CSF and monocyte-derived alveolar macrophage differentiation readouts.
- Outcome frame is protection from allergic airway disease after viral conditioning.

## Caveats

- Gammaherpesvirus biology may differ from acute influenza or rhinovirus.
- This source is not direct evidence for CXCL16/CXCR6 or SLAMF7 mechanisms.
- Allergy-protective outcome is context-specific and should not be collapsed with all reduced ILC2 type 2 function.

## Contradiction and Supersession

- Contradiction/tension: reduced ILC2 type 2 output can be protective here, while ILC2 repair programs can be protective in other viral contexts.
- Supersession status: not superseded; adds a macrophage-instructive ILC2 branch.
