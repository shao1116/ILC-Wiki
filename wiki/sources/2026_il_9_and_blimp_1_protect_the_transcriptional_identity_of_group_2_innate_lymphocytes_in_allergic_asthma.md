## Why It Matters Here

This source directly sharpens the ILC2 asthma model by separating alarmin-driven activation from transcriptional fidelity. It shows that ILC2 type 2 identity requires active stabilization and that loss of Blimp-1 can reroute ILC2s toward type 1 cytokine output while changing allergic pathology.

## Key Findings

- Allergen-associated IL-33 and IL-25 induced IL-9, which upregulated Blimp-1 in ILC2s.
- Blimp-1 restrained type 1 inflammatory programs, including IFN-gamma and TNF, while supporting canonical type 2 cytokine output.
- ILC2 Blimp-1 loss reduced IL-5/IL-13, eosinophil recruitment, and mucus production in the lung in the reported model.
- Blimp-1 loss also increased IL-9 and mast-cell recruitment, showing that loss of type 2 fidelity does not simply mean global ILC2 inactivity.
