    Inflammatory triggers associated with exacerbations of COPD orchestrate plasticity of group 2 innate lymphoid cells in the lungs

    Citation

    • Verified title: Inflammatory triggers associated with exacerbations of COPD orchestrate plasticity of group 2 innate lymphoid cells in the lungs
    • Publication year: 2016
    • DOI: 10.1038/ni.3443
    • Metadata source: crossref-doi (confidence: high)
    • Original local title: Inflammatory triggers associated with exacerbations of COPD orchestrate plasticity of group 2 innate lymphoid cells in the lungs

    Ingest Mode

    • Mode: focused manual crystallization mode
    • Meaning: this source page has been manually upgraded for a priority crystallization pass and may support durable topic/entity/project/digest synthesis.
    • Required boundary: reusable claims from this source still need source-linked species, tissue, assay, model, and confidence labels.

    Source Type

    • primary COPD-trigger and ILC2 plasticity study
    • mouse lung infection/smoke/noxious exposure plus human COPD association and in vitro conversion evidence
    • ILC2-to-ILC1-like plasticity source
    • crystallization status: manually promoted into batch 2 high-priority lung ILC source note

    Evidence Profile

    • Overall confidence: high for lung-resident ILC2 plasticity toward an ILC1-like IFN-gamma/T-bet program under COPD-associated triggers in the reported systems.
    • Directness to this project: very high for COPD/smoke/infection-associated lung ILC2 plasticity.
    • Evidence type: mouse lung models, influenza/viral and noxious stimuli, IL-12/IL-18 cytokine mechanism, T-bet dependence, human COPD association, human ILC2 in vitro conversion.
    • Main biological axis: COPD-associated triggers -> IL-12/IL-18 -> loss of ST2/GATA-3 and gain of T-bet/IFN-gamma -> ILC1-like inflammatory program.

    Why It Matters Here

    This is the strongest batch 2 source for COPD-associated ILC plasticity. It turns ILC2 from a purely type 2 asthma cell into a context-sensitive lung-resident population that can acquire ILC1-like inflammatory behavior.

    Key Findings

    • Lung-resident ILC2s downregulated ST2 and GATA-3 after infectious or noxious COPD-associated stimuli.
    • A T-bet+ IFN-gamma+ ILC1-like population emerged with increased IL-12R and IL-18R expression.
    • IL-12 and IL-18 regulated the conversion, and T-bet-dependent ILC1-like cells augmented virus-induced inflammation.
    • Human ILC2s could be converted by IL-12 toward ILC1 markers in vitro, and COPD patients showed increased circulating ILC1 frequencies that correlated with disease severity and exacerbation susceptibility.

    Claim-Level Confidence

    • High confidence: COPD-associated infectious or noxious triggers can drive lung ILC2 plasticity toward an ILC1-like T-bet/IFN-gamma phenotype in the reported systems.
    • High confidence: IL-12 and IL-18 are key regulatory cues for this ILC2-to-ILC1-like conversion.
    • Medium-high confidence: human data support translational relevance through in vitro conversion and COPD-associated circulating ILC1 correlations.
    • Low confidence: this source does not prove that all COPD ILC1s are derived from ILC2s in human lung tissue.

    Methods and Context

    • Mouse lung-resident ILC flow phenotyping after influenza and COPD-associated stimuli.
    • Cytokine perturbation centered on IL-12, IL-18, ST2, GATA-3, T-bet, and IFN-gamma.
    • Human in vitro ILC2 conversion and COPD patient association data.
    • Best used for plasticity and exacerbation-linked inflammatory context, not stable lineage fate in all humans.

    Caveats

    • Human COPD evidence is associative for circulating ILC1 frequency and severity/exacerbation history.
    • The mouse fate/plasticity mechanism should be labeled separately from human clinical association.
    • ILC2-to-ILC1-like conversion is context-specific and should not erase canonical type 2 ILC2 biology.

    Contradiction and Supersession

    • Contradiction/tension: contrasts with ILC2 repair and type 2 asthma branches by showing a type 1 inflammatory conversion route.
    • Supersession status: not superseded; it defines a COPD/exacerbation-associated plasticity branch.
