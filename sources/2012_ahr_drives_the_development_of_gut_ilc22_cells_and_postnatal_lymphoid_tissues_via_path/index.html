    AHR drives the development of gut ILC22 cells and postnatal lymphoid tissues via pathways dependent on and independent of Notch

    Citation

    • Verified title: AHR drives the development of gut ILC22 cells and postnatal lymphoid tissues via pathways dependent on and independent of Notch
    • Publication year: 2012
    • DOI: 10.1038/ni.2187
    • Metadata source: crossref-doi (confidence: high)
    • Original local title: AHR drives the development of gut ILC22 cells and postnatal lymphoid tissues via pathways dependent on and independent of Notch

    Ingest Mode

    • Mode: focused manual crystallization mode
    • Meaning: this source page was manually upgraded and now supports source-linked AHR-centered ILC3 identity claims.
    • Durable synthesis status: selected high-confidence extrapulmonary ILC3 identity claims from this source were propagated into ILC3 entity and mechanism pages with explicit gut-context labeling.

    Source Type

    • foundational mouse intestinal ILC22 and lymphoid-tissue-development study
    • Evidence profile: Ahr deficiency, IL-22 readouts, intestinal infection outcomes, NKp46-positive versus LTi-like subset comparisons, and Notch-pathway interpretation
    • Knowledge note status: focused source note suitable for conserved ILC3 identity and AHR-pathway framing, not for direct pulmonary disease claims

    Evidence Profile

    • Overall confidence: high for source-specific claims that AHR is required for development or maintenance of gut ILC22 or ILC3-like compartments and their IL-22-associated function.
    • Evidence tags: #cell/ILC3 #tissue/gut #topic/regulation #axis/AHR #axis/IL22 #axis/identity_control #status/focused_crystallization
    • Primary biological axis: AHR is a foundational regulator of ILC22 or ILC3 developmental fitness, with a Notch-linked branch that is especially important for NKp46-positive ILCs.

    Why It Matters Here

    This paper is important because it gives the ILC3 wiki one of its most foundational identity anchors. Even though it is gut-centered, later lung and mucosal ILC3 papers repeatedly rely on the idea that AHR is not optional background biology but a core ILC3 state-maintenance axis.

    Key Findings

    • Ahr-deficient mice showed a major deficit in gut ILC22 cells together with reduced IL-22 production.
    • This deficit impaired protection against intestinal bacterial infection in the reported system.
    • AHR induced a Notch-linked developmental program required for NKp46-positive ILCs.
    • LTi-like ILCs and postnatal lymphoid tissue structures were also AHR dependent, but their Notch dependence was only partial.
    • The source therefore supports AHR as a central developmental and functional regulator across multiple ILC22 or ILC3-like compartments rather than as a minor accessory pathway.

    Claim-Level Confidence

    • High confidence: AHR is required for efficient ILC22 or ILC3-like development and IL-22-associated function in the reported gut models.
    • High confidence: AHR and Notch are mechanistically linked in the NKp46-positive ILC branch.
    • Medium-high confidence: this source provides a conserved identity framework that remains useful for later lung ILC3 interpretation, especially when AHR reappears in pulmonary or mucosal mechanism papers.
    • Low confidence: this paper should not be rewritten as direct evidence for lung ILC3 behavior without matched pulmonary context.

    Methods and Context

    • Species/context: mouse gut mucosal immunity and postnatal lymphoid-tissue development.
    • Assay directness: strong for developmental and infection-linked consequences of AHR loss in the reported intestinal systems.
    • Best wiki use: AHR-centered ILC3 identity, IL-22 biology, and conserved mechanism context for later pulmonary interpretation.

    Caveats

    • This is a gut-focused paper and must stay labeled as such.
    • The terminology predates some later ILC3 naming conventions, so mapping ILC22 terminology to current ILC3 language should be done carefully.
    • The source is best used as an identity and mechanism anchor, not as direct support for lung-disease claims.

    Contradiction and Supersession

    • Contradiction status: this source does not contradict lung ILC3 papers; it supplies the foundational AHR logic that later tissue-specific studies build on.
    • Supersession status: not superseded; it remains a classic reference for AHR-dependent ILC3 or ILC22 biology.
