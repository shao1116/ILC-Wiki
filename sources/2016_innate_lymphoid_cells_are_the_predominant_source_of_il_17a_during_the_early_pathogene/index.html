    Innate Lymphoid Cells Are the Predominant Source of IL-17A during the Early Pathogenesis of Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome

    Citation

    • Verified title: Innate Lymphoid Cells Are the Predominant Source of IL-17A during the Early Pathogenesis of Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome
    • Publication year: 2016
    • DOI: 10.1164/rccm.201410-1782oc
    • Metadata source: crossref-doi (confidence: high)
    • Original local title: Innate Lymphoid Cells Are the Predominant Source of IL-17A during the Early Pathogenesis of Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome

    Source Type

    • primary research article
    • mouse acute lung injury/ARDS-pathogenesis model
    • pulmonary ILC3 IL-17A source paper
    • crystallization status: manually promoted into batch 1 high-priority lung ILC source note

    Evidence Profile

    • Overall confidence: high for pulmonary ILC3s as an early major IL-17A source in the reported mouse lung injury models.
    • Directness to this project: high for ARDS/lung injury and ILC3 IL-17A pathogenesis.
    • Evidence type: aerosolized LPS and Pseudomonas aeruginosa lung injury, IL-17A knockout comparison, RORgammat+ cell phenotyping, RAG2-deficient context.
    • Main biological axis: acute lung injury -> pILC3/RORgammat+ innate lymphoid IL-17A -> neutrophil recruitment and early immunopathogenesis.

    Why It Matters Here

    This is a key source for the pathogenic IL-17A-producing pulmonary ILC3 branch. It counterbalances IL-22 protective ILC3 infection literature and supports disease-phase-specific interpretation.

    Key Findings

    • IL-17A knockout mice showed markedly reduced neutrophil infiltration after lung injury compared with wild-type mice.
    • Most RORgammat+ cells in the mouse lung at the early injury time point were identified as group 3 innate lymphoid cells.
    • Pulmonary ILC3s were described as distinct from gut ILC3s.
    • RAG2-deficient mice lacking T cells but retaining ILCs did not show ameliorated pathology, supporting a non-T-cell IL-17A source.

    Claim-Level Confidence

    • High confidence: IL-17A contributes to early neutrophilic lung injury pathogenesis in these mouse models.
    • High confidence: pulmonary ILC3s are a major early IL-17A source in the source's experimental context.
    • Medium confidence: this source is relevant to ARDS biology, but human ARDS translation should be separately validated.
    • Low confidence: this source should not be used to claim all lung ILC3 activity is pathogenic, because IL-22-producing ILC3s can support bacterial defense.

    Methods and Context

    • Mouse lung injury induced by aerosolized LPS or Pseudomonas aeruginosa infection.
    • IL-17A knockout and RAG2-deficient settings used to separate cytokine and adaptive immunity contributions.
    • Flow cytometric phenotyping of RORgammat+ pulmonary cells.
    • Early lung injury/neutrophil recruitment frame.

    Caveats

    • Mouse acute lung injury models do not fully recapitulate all human ARDS etiologies.
    • ILC3 pathogenicity is time- and cytokine-specific.
    • The source supports IL-17A-focused pathology, not a general anti-ILC3 conclusion.

    Contradiction and Supersession

    • Contradiction/tension: contrasts with IL-22-focused ILC3 defense in Streptococcus pneumoniae infection.
    • Supersession status: not superseded; best treated as the early IL-17A lung injury branch.
