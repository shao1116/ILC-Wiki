    Innate Lymphoid Cells Are Required to Induce Airway Hyperreactivity in a Murine Neutrophilic Asthma Model

    Citation

    • Verified title: Innate Lymphoid Cells Are Required to Induce Airway Hyperreactivity in a Murine Neutrophilic Asthma Model
    • Publication year: 2022
    • DOI: 10.3389/fimmu.2022.849155
    • Metadata source: crossref-doi (confidence: high)
    • Original local title: Innate Lymphoid Cells Are Required to Induce Airway Hyperreactivity in a Murine Neutrophilic Asthma Model

    Ingest Mode

    • Mode: focused manual crystallization mode
    • Meaning: this source page has been manually upgraded for a priority crystallization pass and may support durable topic/entity/project/digest synthesis.
    • Required boundary: reusable claims from this source still need source-linked species, tissue, assay, model, and confidence labels.

    Source Type

    • primary murine neutrophilic asthma model study
    • low-dose LPS airway exposure model with ILC transfer and cytokine perturbation
    • ILC requirement for non-allergic neutrophilic AHR source
    • crystallization status: manually promoted into batch 2 high-priority lung ILC source note

    Evidence Profile

    • Overall confidence: high for ILC requirement in LPS-induced AHR in the reported murine neutrophilic asthma model.
    • Directness to this project: high for neutrophilic asthma and airway hyperreactivity; medium for ILC3 specificity because the source frames ILCs broadly and reports increased lung ILC3.
    • Evidence type: mouse low-dose LPS exposure, BAL neutrophilia, lung cytokines/chemokines, SCID and Rag2-/- gammaC-/- contexts, ILC adoptive transfer, IL-1beta/IL-17A/neutrophil perturbation.
    • Main biological axis: repeated airway LPS -> lung ILC3 increase and IL-1beta/IL-17A/IL-22/KC/MIP-2 milieu -> ILC-dependent AHR in a neutrophilic model.

    Why It Matters Here

    This source strengthens the non-eosinophilic asthma branch by showing that ILCs are functionally required for AHR in a murine neutrophilic model, while keeping the claim broader than ILC3-only when the transfer evidence is not subset-exclusive.

    Key Findings

    • Repeated low-dose LPS exposure induced AHR and BAL neutrophilia in BALB/c mice.
    • The model increased lung ILC3, KC, MIP-2, IL-1beta, IL-17A, IL-22, and TNF.
    • Adoptive transfer of ILCs into Rag2-/- gammaC-/- mice supported a causal role for ILCs in AHR induction.
    • IL-1beta antagonism, but not IL-17A or neutrophil targeting, partially reduced LPS-induced AHR in the reported model.

    Claim-Level Confidence

    • High confidence: ILCs are required to induce AHR in this murine low-dose LPS neutrophilic asthma model.
    • High confidence: repeated airway LPS increases lung ILC3 and a neutrophilic inflammatory cytokine/chemokine milieu in this model.
    • Medium confidence: IL-1beta contributes to AHR in this model, but partial reduction indicates additional pathways are involved.
    • Low confidence: the source should not be cited as proving a single ILC3-only mechanism for human neutrophilic asthma.

    Methods and Context

    • Endonasal low-dose LPS exposure on four consecutive days.
    • Mouse strains included wild-type BALB/c, SCID, IL-17A-/-, and Rag2-/- gammaC-/- mice.
    • Readouts included methacholine AHR, BAL neutrophilia, lung ILC subsets, cytokines, and chemokines.
    • Adoptive ILC transfer used to test functional requirement.

    Caveats

    • Murine low-dose LPS is a model for non-allergic neutrophilic asthma, not a full substitute for human disease.
    • ILC3 increase and ILC requirement should be kept distinct unless subset-specific causality is directly shown.
    • IL-17A was measured and genetically tested, but IL-17A blockade/deficiency was not the dominant AHR-rescue mechanism in the abstract-level evidence.

    Contradiction and Supersession

    • Contradiction/tension: complements human ILC3/neutrophilic asthma sources while showing an IL-1beta-sensitive, not purely IL-17A-dependent, AHR mechanism.
    • Supersession status: not superseded; use as a murine non-allergic neutrophilic AHR model.
