    Insulin-like Growth Factor 1 Supports a Pulmonary Niche that Promotes Type 3 Innate Lymphoid Cell Development in Newborn Lungs

    Evidence Profile

    • Overall confidence: high for alveolar fibroblast-derived IGF1 supporting postnatal pulmonary ILC3 development in the reported mouse system.
    • Directness to this project: high for developmental lung ILC3 niche biology; medium for adult inflammatory lung disease.
    • Evidence type: developmental lung niche mapping, conditional IGF1/IGF1R perturbation, pneumonia susceptibility, human premature infant/BPD comparison.
    • Main biological axis: alveolar fibroblast IGF1 -> ILC precursor expansion/maturation -> pulmonary ILC3 development -> neonatal antibacterial defense.

    Why It Matters Here

    This source anchors the tissue-niche branch of lung ILC3 biology. It shows that pulmonary ILC3 abundance and function are shaped by developmental stromal signals, not only by inflammatory cytokines during disease.

    Key Findings

    • Pulmonary ILC3s descended from ILC precursors occupying a spatially distinct developing-lung niche.
    • Alveolar fibroblast-derived IGF1 instructed postnatal expansion and maturation of pulmonary ILC precursors.
    • Conditional loss of fibroblast IGF1 or ILC precursor IGF1 receptor disrupted pulmonary ILC3 biogenesis and increased newborn mouse susceptibility to pneumonia.
    • Premature infants with bronchopulmonary dysplasia showed reduced IGF1 and pulmonary ILC3 numbers in the source text.

    Claim-Level Confidence

    • High confidence: alveolar fibroblast-derived IGF1 supports neonatal pulmonary ILC3 development in this source.
    • High confidence: the source links lung developmental programs with mucosal immune defense against pneumonia.
    • Medium confidence: human premature infant/BPD observations support translational relevance but do not by themselves establish causality in humans.
    • Low confidence: adult asthma or ARDS claims should not be inferred from this source without additional adult disease evidence.

    Methods and Context

    • Developmental mouse lung niche analysis.
    • Conditional manipulation of IGF1 or IGF1 receptor pathways.
    • Pneumonia susceptibility as functional readout.
    • Human premature infant/BPD comparison included as translational context.
    • Note: the local filename year appears inconsistent with the article text, which identifies a 2020 Immunity paper.

    Caveats

    • Strongest causal evidence is in neonatal mouse lung.
    • Human data should be treated as supportive association unless manually reviewed in full detail.
    • Developmental niche biology should not be merged automatically with adult inflammatory fibroblast-ILC3 axes.

    Contradiction and Supersession

    • Contradiction/tension: developmental IGF1 niche support differs from inflammatory SCF/KIT fibroblast-ILC3 activation in neutrophilic asthma.
    • Supersession status: not superseded; best treated as the neonatal developmental niche branch.
