    Lipid-Droplet Formation Drives Pathogenic Group 2 Innate Lymphoid Cells in Airway Inflammation

    Citation

    • Verified title: Lipid-Droplet Formation Drives Pathogenic Group 2 Innate Lymphoid Cells in Airway Inflammation
    • Publication year: 2020
    • DOI: 10.1016/j.immuni.2020.03.003
    • Metadata source: crossref-doi (confidence: high)
    • Original local title: Lipid-Droplet Formation Drives Pathogenic Group 2 Innate Lymphoid Cells in Airway Inflammation

    Ingest Mode

    • Mode: focused manual crystallization mode
    • Meaning: this source page was manually upgraded and now supports source-linked ILC2 metabolic-state claims.
    • Durable synthesis status: selected high-confidence pathogenic-metabolism claims from this source were propagated into ILC2 entity/topic/disease synthesis.

    Source Type

    • primary mouse airway-inflammation and ILC2 metabolism study
    • Evidence profile: chronic allergen-driven airway inflammation, nutrient-tracing and metabolic analyses, lipid-droplet biology, PPARg/DGAT1 regulation, mTOR-linked control, and dietary intervention
    • Knowledge note status: focused source note suitable for metabolic-pathogenic-state interpretation with preclinical boundaries preserved

    Evidence Profile

    • Overall confidence: high for source-specific claims that pathogenic airway ILC2 responses require external lipid uptake and lipid-droplet biology in the reported mouse system.
    • Evidence tags: #cell/ILC2 #tissue/lung #topic/regulation #topic/pulmonary_disease #axis/metabolism #axis/type2_immunity #status/focused_crystallization
    • Primary biological axis: chronic airway-activated ILC2s use lipid uptake, transient lipid-droplet storage, and phospholipid synthesis to sustain pathogenic proliferation and inflammation.

    Why It Matters Here

    This source gives the wiki a more precise metabolic model for pathogenic ILC2 states. Instead of saying only that metabolism matters, it identifies a specific airway-inflammation program in which IL-33, mTOR, PPARg, DGAT1, and lipid-droplet formation cooperate to sustain proliferative pathogenic ILC2 responses.

    Key Findings

    • Chronically activated airway ILC2s increased uptake of both external lipids and glucose in the reported allergen-driven model.
    • External fatty acids were transiently stored in lipid droplets and converted into phospholipids that supported ILC2 proliferation.
    • IL-33 imprinted this metabolic program, and Pparg and Dgat1 were important regulators of lipid uptake and lipid-droplet formation.
    • Glucose availability and mTOR signaling regulated this pathogenic metabolic state.
    • Restricting dietary glucose with a ketogenic diet markedly reduced ILC2-driven airway inflammation in the reported preclinical system.

    Claim-Level Confidence

    • High confidence: pathogenic airway ILC2 responses in this source require lipid uptake and transient lipid-droplet formation.
    • High confidence: IL-33, PPARg, DGAT1, and mTOR are central regulators of the reported pathogenic ILC2 metabolic program.
    • Medium-high confidence: ketogenic-diet intervention supports metabolic tractability in this model, but it remains preclinical and context-specific.
    • Low confidence: this source should not be used alone to generalize ketogenic-diet or metabolic-intervention claims to human asthma care.

    Methods and Context

    • Species/context: mouse chronic allergen-driven airway inflammation.
    • Assay directness: strong for metabolic mechanism because the source combined functional airway-inflammation readouts with cellular metabolic measurements.
    • Best wiki use: pathogenic ILC2 metabolism, proliferative state control, and linkage between nutrient handling and airway inflammation.

    Caveats

    • Evidence is strongest in mouse airway-inflammation models and needs human validation.
    • The metabolic program described here is for chronic pathogenic activation, not necessarily steady-state or reparative ILC2s.
    • Diet-intervention claims should remain explicitly preclinical.

    Contradiction and Supersession

    • Contradiction status: this source refines, rather than contradicts, broader ILC2 metabolic literature by identifying a specific lipid-droplet-dependent pathogenic branch.
    • Supersession status: not superseded; it remains a key mechanistic source for pathogenic ILC2 metabolism in airway inflammation.
