    Cigarette smoke aggravates asthma by inducing memory-like type 3 innate lymphoid cells¶

    Citation¶

    • Verified title: Cigarette smoke aggravates asthma by inducing memory-like type 3 innate lymphoid cells
    • Publication year: 2022
    • DOI: 10.1038/s41467-022-31491-1
    • Metadata source: crossref-doi (confidence: high)
    • Original local title: Cigarette smoke aggravates asthma by inducing memory-like type 3 innate lymphoid cells

    Ingest Mode¶

    • Mode: focused manual crystallization mode
    • Meaning: this source page has been manually upgraded for a priority crystallization pass and may support durable topic/entity/project/digest synthesis.
    • Required boundary: reusable claims from this source still need source-linked species, tissue, assay, model, and confidence labels.

    Source Type¶

    • primary human asthma and cigarette-smoke ILC3 study
    • paired sputum and blood analysis with in vitro cigarette-smoke-extract experiments
    • smoke-associated memory-like ILC3 source
    • crystallization status: manually promoted into batch 2 high-priority lung ILC source note

    Evidence Profile¶

    • Overall confidence: high for smoking-associated increases in sputum pro-inflammatory NCR- ILC3s and blood CD45RO+ ILC3s in asthma subjects as reported.
    • Directness to this project: very high for smoke-associated ILC3/neutrophilic asthma biology.
    • Evidence type: human sputum and blood flow cytometry, clinical correlation, in vitro cigarette smoke extract exposure, airway epithelial barrier/IL-1beta response.
    • Main biological axis: cigarette smoke in asthma -> epithelial barrier impairment and IL-1beta -> activated/memory-like ILC3 -> neutrophil/M1 macrophage-associated non-allergic inflammation.

    Why It Matters Here¶

    This is the key smoke-asthma ILC3 source in the wiki. It connects smoking, sputum ILC3, CD45RO+ memory-like ILC3s, neutrophil-rich inflammation, M1 macrophage association, and epithelial IL-1beta.

    Key Findings¶

    • Smoking asthma subjects had higher frequencies of pro-inflammatory NCR- ILC3s in induced sputum than non-smoking asthma subjects.
    • Smoking was associated with CD45RO-expressing memory-like ILC3s in blood.
    • ILC3 frequencies correlated with smoking amount, disease severity, circulating neutrophils, and M1 alveolar macrophage frequencies, but not eosinophil frequencies.
    • Cigarette smoke extract induced CD45RO expression in ILC3s in vitro and impaired airway epithelial barrier function while increasing epithelial IL-1beta production.

    Claim-Level Confidence¶

    • High confidence: cigarette smoking in asthma is associated with increased sputum NCR- ILC3s and blood CD45RO+ memory-like ILC3s in this cohort.
    • High confidence: the ILC3 signal aligns more with neutrophil/M1 macrophage-associated non-allergic inflammation than with eosinophilic allergy in this source.
    • Medium-high confidence: cigarette smoke extract can promote CD45RO expression in ILC3s and epithelial IL-1beta-associated activation signals in vitro.
    • Low confidence: the source does not by itself prove that ILC3s are the sole causal driver of smoking-aggravated asthma severity in patients.

    Methods and Context¶

    • Paired sputum and blood samples from smoking and non-smoking asthma subjects, plus healthy smoker/non-smoker comparison groups.
    • ILC subset flow cytometry; ILC3s include NCR- and CD45RO+ memory-like compartments.
    • In vitro cigarette smoke extract experiments with ILCs and airway epithelial cells.
    • Best used for human association plus ex vivo/in vitro mechanism, not for definitive clinical intervention claims.

    Caveats¶

    • Clinical associations do not prove direction of causality.
    • CD45RO is interpreted as memory-like/activation-associated in ILCs; its exact functional meaning is less established than in T cells.
    • Sputum and blood ILC3 compartments should not automatically be treated as identical to resident lung tissue ILC3s.

    Contradiction and Supersession¶

    • Contradiction/tension: reinforces an inflammatory ILC3 branch, in contrast to protective IL-22 ILC3 defense sources.
    • Supersession status: not superseded; use as a smoke-aggravated asthma branch.
